# ALMA Content Priorities

## Mission
ALMA (Adaptive Learning & Measurement Architecture) is a **youth justice intelligence system** focused on building the evidence base for **diversion, early intervention, and community-based alternatives to detention** for young people in Australia, with particular focus on Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander youth.

## Priority Content Categories

### 1. CORE PRIORITY: Youth Justice Research & Policy
- Youth justice system reports (AIHW, state departments)
- Detention statistics and outcomes data
- Diversion program evaluations
- Justice reinvestment studies
- Closing the Gap youth justice targets
- Royal Commission findings (youth detention, child protection)
- Youth justice legislation and policy documents

### 2. HIGH PRIORITY: Evidence-Based Interventions
- Diversion programs (police, court, pre-sentence)
- Therapeutic interventions for young offenders
- Family-based interventions
- Cultural connection programs for Indigenous youth
- Education and employment pathways
- Restorative justice programs
- Bail support and supervision alternatives

### 3. HIGH PRIORITY: Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander Focus
- Aboriginal-led justice programs
- Deaths in custody reports and inquiries
- Over-representation research and solutions
- Community-controlled organization programs
- Cultural authority in justice settings
- Self-determination in youth justice

### 4. MEDIUM PRIORITY: Related Systems
- Child protection intersections with justice
- Out-of-home care to justice pipeline
- Mental health services for justice-involved youth
- Disability support for justice-involved youth
- Education re-engagement programs
- Housing and homelessness interventions

### 5. MEDIUM PRIORITY: Funding & Economics
- Cost-benefit analyses of diversion vs detention
- Justice reinvestment funding models
- Government budget allocations (youth justice)
- Philanthropic funding for justice programs

## Content Exclusions (Low/No Priority)
- General health information (immunisation, nutrition, etc.)
- Adult justice system (unless transition-related)
- General parenting advice (unless justice-specific)
- Tourism or cultural heritage (unless justice program)
- General Aboriginal health (unless justice intersection)
- COVID-19 vaccination campaigns
- Hospital/rehabilitation services (unless justice-specific)

## Priority Source Domains

### Government (Tier 1)
- aihw.gov.au (Australian Institute of Health and Welfare)
- justice.vic.gov.au, communities.qld.gov.au, etc. (state youth justice)
- royalcommission.gov.au (relevant inquiries)
- pc.gov.au (Productivity Commission - ROGS)
- closingthegap.gov.au

### Research (Tier 1)
- aic.gov.au (Australian Institute of Criminology)
- bocsar.nsw.gov.au (NSW Bureau of Crime Statistics)
- csc.gov.au (Criminology Research Grants)
- University criminology/justice research centres

### Aboriginal Community Controlled (Tier 1)
- als.org.au (Aboriginal Legal Service)
- naccho.org.au (health intersection)
- First Nations justice organizations

### Advocacy & Policy (Tier 2)
- humanrights.gov.au
- smartjustice.org.au
- justicereforminitiative.org.au
- changethereord.org.au
- amnesty.org.au (Indigenous rights)

### Media (Tier 2 - for context)
- Guardian Australia (Indigenous affairs, justice)
- ABC News (justice reporting)
- The Conversation (research summaries)

## Relevance Keywords (for filtering)

### Include if contains:
- youth justice, juvenile justice, young offender
- detention, custody, incarceration (youth context)
- diversion, bail support, supervised release
- recidivism, reoffending, desistance
- restorative justice, conferencing
- justice reinvestment
- child protection AND justice
- Aboriginal AND (justice OR custody OR detention OR diversion)
- deaths in custody
- closing the gap AND justice
- early intervention AND (offending OR crime OR justice)

### Exclude if primarily about:
- immunisation, vaccination
- hospital services, rehabilitation (medical)
- dental, nutrition, fitness
- aged care, disability (general)
- tourism, cultural events
- COVID-19 health advice
- general parenting (non-justice)

## Quality Indicators

### High-Value Content
- Evaluation reports with outcomes data
- Statistical reports with Indigenous breakdowns
- Program descriptions with evidence ratings
- Cost-benefit analyses
- Royal Commission findings
- Aboriginal community voice/authority

### Lower-Value Content
- General promotional content
- News without new data
- Opinion without evidence
- Duplicated content

# ALMA + JusticeHub Revenue Model
## How Money Flows From Funders → Communities & Knowledge Holders

**Date**: January 1, 2026
**Status**: Ready to pitch

---

## The Core Problem We Solve

**Traditional Model** (Extractive):
```
Government/Philanthropy → University Research → Academic Papers (paywalled)
                                              ↓
                                    Communities get cited
                                    Communities get $0
                                    Knowledge extracted, not valued
```

**ALMA + JusticeHub Model** (Regenerative):
```
Government/Philanthropy → JusticeHub Platform → Intelligence Commons (open)
                                              ↓
                                    Communities own intelligence
                                    Communities get revenue share
                                    Knowledge valued, compensated
```

## Revenue Streams: How We Get Paid

### 1. State Government Intelligence Licenses ($400K/year by Year 3)

**What They Buy**:
- Annual subscription to ALMA intelligence for their state
- Evidence base for policy development
- Outcome tracking for budget justification
- National benchmarking (compare their state to others)
- Quarterly intelligence updates (new programs ingested)

**Pricing Model**:
- **Tier 1 States** (QLD, NSW, VIC): $75K/year (large populations, high budgets)
- **Tier 2 States** (WA, SA, NT): $50K/year (medium populations)
- **Tier 3 States** (TAS, ACT): $25K/year (small populations)

> - Benchmark against other states

**Why They Pay**:
- **Cheaper than commissioning research** ($75K vs $200-500K)
- **Faster than traditional research** (quarterly updates vs 18-month studies)
- **More comprehensive** (national context, not just QLD in isolation)
- **Actionable intelligence** (portfolio scores, evidence validation)

**Revenue Share to Communities**:

  - QATSICPP gets share (their 3 programs documented)

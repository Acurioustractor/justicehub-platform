This fellowship proposal positions **you** as a practice-led researcher exploring how **digital storytelling platforms (JusticeHub) and immersive community art installations (Contained)** can work together to create the conditions for systems change in youth justice and community self-determination.

**The Core Proposition:** Communities hold the knowledge, stories, and solutions to break cycles of disadvantage. But systemic barriersâ€”in hearts & minds, resource allocation, and policyâ€”prevent community wisdom from shifting practice. This fellowship researches how **dual-mode engagement** (physical installation + digital platform) can bridge the gap between lived experience and systems change.

## The Research Idea: Pioneering Work

### What Makes This Innovative?

**1. Integration of Physical and Digital for Systems Change**
- No existing research combines immersive art installations with AI-assisted digital storytelling platforms
- Tests hypothesis: **Multi-modal engagement** (embodied experience + ongoing digital connection) creates deeper systems change than either alone

**2. Story as Research Methodology**
- Challenges conventional "researcher studies community" model
- Positions communities as co-researchers, with story as primary evidence
- Tests: Can AI-assisted story extraction honor lived experience while accelerating knowledge dissemination?

**3. Economic Models for Community Knowledge**
- **JusticeHub's** revenue-sharing framework treats community stories as intellectual property deserving compensation
- Research question: Does economic participation in storytelling shift power dynamics in systems change?

**4. Reversing the Evidence Paradigm**
- **Contained** shows that "evidence" (statistics, cost comparisons) becomes meaningful when combined with story and embodied experience
- Tests: How does physical immersion in comparative evidence change hearts & minds differently than reports?

### What Gap Does This Fill?

**Current Gap:**
- Youth justice research focuses on programs OR policy OR public perception
- Community storytelling platforms focus on awareness OR advocacy
- Art installations focus on experience OR education
- **None integrate all three** + **test whether integration creates conditions for systems change**

**This Fellowship Fills:**
- How physical + digital storytelling creates conditions for policy shift
- How community ownership of narrative infrastructure affects resource allocation
- How lived experience becomes evidence that moves decision-makers
- How art + technology + story work together to shift hearts, minds, and systems

## Methodology: How Will You Explore This Idea?

### Phase 1: Establish Baseline (Months 1-3)
**Physical Component (Contained):**
- Launch installation in 3 communities (targeting areas with high youth incarceration rates)
- Document visitor responses, policy-maker engagement, media coverage
- Track: Who attends? What shifts in their understanding? What actions do they take?

**Digital Component (JusticeHub):**
- Implement AI-assisted storytelling pipeline with 10 community organizations
- Build ownership/revenue-sharing framework
- Document: How do storytellers experience the process? What barriers exist?

**Integration Research:**
- Test hypothesis: Does visiting Contained increase engagement with JusticeHub stories?
- Track: Do people who experience physical installation become digital community members?

### Phase 2: Deep Community Engagement (Months 4-9)
**Co-Research with Communities:**
- Partner with 5 communities hosting Contained
- Conduct story-gathering workshops where young people and families share experiences
- Use JusticeHub AI to extract themes, quotes, case studies from transcripts
- **Critical Step:** Communities review, edit, and approve extracted stories
- Document: How does AI-assisted extraction compare to traditional research methods in honoring lived experience?

**Systems Mapping:**
- Map the ecosystem around youth justice: decision-makers, funders, service providers, media
- Track how stories move through this system
- Document: Which storytelling formats (digital article, video, installation testimony) reach which audiences?

**Economic Modeling:**
- Implement revenue-sharing when stories are used by media, policymakers, researchers
- Document: How does financial participation affect community engagement? Trust? Agency?

### Phase 3: Testing Systems Change Hypotheses (Months 10-12)
**Policy Impact:**
- Work with communities to present findings to:
  - State/federal policymakers
  - Youth justice departments
  - Funding bodies
- Document: How do policymakers respond to community-generated evidence vs traditional research?

**Hearts & Minds Shift:**
- Measure: Media coverage, public discourse analysis, social media sentiment
- Track: How does narrative change from "tough on crime" to "community support"?

**Resource Flow:**
- Document: Does evidence of community solutions shift funding from detention to community programs?
- Track: Do organizations using JusticeHub gain new funding opportunities?

### Phase 4: Synthesis and Dissemination (Months 13-18)
**Months 13-15: Analysis**
- What conditions enabled systems change? (or prevented it?)
- How did physical + digital integration work (or not work)?
- What role did community ownership play?
- How did multiple knowledge systems (lived experience, creative practice, data) interact?

**Months 16-18: Dissemination**
- **For Communities:** Open-source playbook: "How to Host Contained + Build Your JusticeHub"
- **For Funders:** "Investing in Community Knowledge Infrastructure: Revenue-Sharing Models That Work"
- **For Policymakers:** "Evidence from the Inside: Why Community-Led Research Changes Policy"
- **For Sector:** "Beyond Storytelling: When Digital Platforms Become Sites of Self-Determination"

## Addressing Potential Concerns

### "Isn't this just service delivery + tech platform?"

**Response:**
NO. This is research into the **conditions** needed for systems change:
- Not delivering services, but researching how to shift systems that determine service delivery
- Not building a platform, but researching how platform ownership affects community agency
- Not creating an installation, but researching how physical + digital integration shifts policy

**The Research Questions Are:**
1. How do different knowledge systems (lived experience, creative practice, AI-assisted analysis, evidence) work together for systems change?
2. Does community ownership of digital infrastructure shift power dynamics in policy influence?
3. What combination of immersive experience and ongoing digital engagement creates lasting hearts & minds change?
4. How can economic participation in storytelling enable community self-determination?

### "Is this too broad? Too many moving parts?"

**Response:**
The integration IS the innovation:
- **Contained alone** = powerful experience, but temporary
- **JusticeHub alone** = useful platform, but lacks embodied connection
- **Together** = Test whether multi-modal creates deeper, more sustained systems change

**The fellowship researches the integration itself:**
- Does visiting Contained increase JusticeHub engagement?
- Do digital stories make people want physical experience?
- How do they work together to move hearts, minds, policy?

### "How is this different from other storytelling projects?"

**Response:**
Three key differences:

1. **Economic model:** Storytellers receive revenue share when their stories create value (unique in Australia)
2. **AI for community agency:** Using AI to accelerate story extraction, but communities control editing/approval (tests whether tech can serve decolonization)
3. **Systems change focus:** Not just raising awareness, but researching how storytelling infrastructure shifts policies, resources, and hearts/minds

### "Will communities actually participate?"

**Response:**
Already happening:
- Contained is designed to be hosted by communities (launching Oct 2025)
- JusticeHub has existing community partnerships
- Fellowship formalizes and researches what's already emerging organically

**Plus:**
- Communities are compensated for participation
- Research serves community goals (policy change) not just academic outputs
- Co-research model means communities shape the questions

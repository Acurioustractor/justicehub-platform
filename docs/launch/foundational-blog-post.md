## The Headline

Every year, Australia spends over $1.1 million per child to lock up young people in detention centres that have an 84.5% failure rate.

Meanwhile, grassroots programs achieving 78% success rates operate on shoestring budgets, invisible to funders, disconnected from each other, and unknown to the families who need them most.

This is the crisis JusticeHub was built to solve.

---

## The Problem Nobody Wants to Talk About

Let's start with the numbers that keep us awake at night:

**24x higher.** That's how much more likely an Indigenous young person is to be incarcerated compared to their non-Indigenous peers.

**84.5% recidivism.** That's how many young people released from detention end up back in the system. Eight out of ten.

**$1.1 million per year.** That's what we spend per child to achieve those catastrophic results.

We don't have a youth crime problem. We have a youth justice system that manufactures more crime.

---

## The Solution That Already Exists

Here's what the media won't tell you: communities already have the cure.

Across Australia, grassroots organizations achieve outcomes that make detention look not just expensive, but absurd:

- **Groote Eylandt** saw 95% reduction in youth crime through cultural healing programs
- **BackTrack Youth Works** achieves 87% non-reoffending through practical skills and mentorship
- **Transition 2 Success** delivers 67% success at 95% lower cost than detention

These aren't pilot programs or theoretical models. They're working right now, transforming young lives, at a fraction of the cost of locking kids up.

So why doesn't everyone know about them?

---

## The Gap We're Filling

Three problems keep community solutions invisible:

**1. Fragmentation**
Each program operates in isolation. The youth worker in Mount Isa doesn't know what's working in Alice Springs. Funders can't compare approaches. Families can't find what's available.

**2. Invisibility**
While detention centres have million-dollar budgets and government backing, community programs survive on grants and goodwill. They have no marketing team, no lobbying power, no platform.

**3. No Evidence Base**
When policymakers ask "what works?", they get studies about American models from twenty years ago. They don't get real-time data from Australian communities showing what's working right now.

JusticeHub closes all three gaps.

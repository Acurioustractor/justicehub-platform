# Justice Reinvestment — Per-Site Gap Report

_2026-06-13._

## Why gaps exist

"The gaps are overwhelmingly a function of program age and the evaluation calendar, not poor research. Two-thirds of these sites were funded in the 2024-2025 Commonwealth rounds and are in establishment/backbone/baseline phase; the Attorney-General's Department itself states the first evaluations under the national JR framework do not begin until later in 2026. So the absence of outcome metrics is the expected, honest state of an early program — only Maranguka (2018 KPMG) and Groote Eylandt have had time to produce real evaluations, and even Maranguka's report carries its own caveat that it measures a single year of change, not a sustained causal trend. DATA gaps: per-site funding figures are withheld at the government level (only national envelopes are published) and the systems that hold the awarded values (GrantConnect, communitygrants.gov.au) return 403 to ad-hoc fetches, so the figure exists but is gated. CAPACITY gaps: many sites are small Aboriginal corporations or council-auspiced initiatives running on planning-phase grants with no communications staff, which is why so many lack a standalone website, a dedicated JR page, a logo asset, or any self-published news — the public record is almost entirely government press releases and NIT articles written about them, not by them. GOVERNANCE and CULTURAL-SAFETY gaps are deliberate, not deficiencies: people arrays are thin or empty at numerous remote and survivor-led sites (Ngurratjuta, Cherbourg, Ceduna, Carnarvon, Mampu-Maninjaku, Kinchela) because naming individuals without community sign-off, or where Sorry Business may apply, is unsafe — withholding is the correct call and the platform must support self-determined naming rather than scrape it. REMOTENESS compounds all of the above in the NT and remote WA/QLD, where thin online presence reflects distance from media and digital infrastructure more than inactivity. Finally, a structural data-model gap on JusticeHub's side amplifies the harm: with no 'metric class' field, contextual baselines, enforcement-program figures, and actuarial projections sit alongside genuine outcomes and risk being read as JR results — the most fixable gap in the list, and the one most likely to mislead funders and community."

## Network-level gaps

- OUTCOME EVIDENCE is the network-wide gap: only Maranguka (Bourke) and Groote Eylandt have genuine primary-source outcome evaluations. 18 of 28 sites have an empty impact_metrics array and every site has empty/absent verified_metrics. The network can show activity and funding but almost nowhere can it show independently-evaluated justice outcomes.
- Queensland is thin on connective tissue and evidence: Cherbourg (null logo + null website), Yarrabah, Doomadgee, Mornington Island, Napranum — all moderate-to-thin, all early-stage, none with JR-specific outcome data. QLD sites also lack a visible state-level coordinating body equivalent to NSW's Just Reinvest, SA's JRSA or WA's Social Reinvestment WA.
- Northern Territory remote sites are the thinnest cluster: Central Australia (thin), Ngurratjuta/Papunya-Mt Liebig-Haasts Bluff (thin), Lajamanu (no logo), Maningrida, Katherine, Mampu-Maninjaku — almost all establishment-stage with no metrics, weak/placeholder logos, and several with empty people arrays. Remoteness compounds data scarcity here more than anywhere else.
- South Australia has only two sites in the set (Tiraapendi Wodli, KNYA) plus the consultation-stage Ceduna; JRSA as the state network was repeatedly unreachable (ECONNREFUSED), so the SA connective tissue exists but is effectively invisible to the platform.
- Establishment-stage sites (2024-2025 federal rounds) dominate and share an identical gap signature: funding announced, no allocation figure disclosed, no evaluation, often no named local coordinator. This is a program-type gap (planning/backbone phase) rather than a geographic one — the platform currently has little to show for a site between 'funded' and 'first evaluation (late 2026)'.
- PER-SITE FUNDING FIGURES are missing network-wide: not one record could find the dollar amount allocated to its specific site — only the national $79M/$109M/$69M/$81.5M envelopes. GrantConnect/communitygrants.gov.au hold these but block ad-hoc fetches.
- MISATTRIBUTION RISK is a connective-tissue gap: related-but-distinct programs bleed into JR profiles (Moree's policing/Operation Mongoose figures, Nowra's Circle Sentencing, Mornington Island's MIRJ/Junkuri Laka, AMHC's actuarial projections, Cherbourg's 2017 cost baseline, Derby's Target 120). Without a metric-class field the network conflates enforcement and projection figures with community-led JR outcomes.
- ENTITY DISAMBIGUATION is missing: the network has multiple confusable bodies that must not be merged — three 'Kurdiji' entities; Ebenezer vs ALSWA's Old Ways New Ways both in Balga; Ngurratjuta vs Mampu-Maninjaku in Central Australia; Tiraapendi Wodli vs Ceduna in SA. No structured relationship layer currently prevents bad joins.
- State/advocacy networks (Just Reinvest NSW, JRSA, Social Reinvestment WA, JRNA) are referenced everywhere but not modelled as a layer — the map shows sites as dots without the coordinating bodies and sibling-site links that make it a network.

## Capacity supports JusticeHub could offer

- Site-owned impact-metric intake: a simple structured form (metric / value / as-reported / source URL / year / 'outcome vs context vs projection' tag) that lets each site log its own figures with provenance, so JusticeHub never has to borrow or guess. This directly fixes the #1 gap — 18 of 28 sites have zero impact metrics.
- A 'metric class' label baked into the data model — outcome / process-output / projection / contextual-baseline / borrowed-from-related-program — rendered visibly on every figure. This is the single most important integrity fix: it stops Moree's policing $, Nowra's Circle Sentencing, Mornington Island's MIRJ, Cherbourg's 2017 cost figure and AMHC's $70M projection from reading as JR outcomes, and it protects the genuinely-evaluated Maranguka/Groote figures by contrast.
- Per-site source/provenance sidecar (the .provenance.md pattern already used in this repo) auto-generated from the figure intake, so every dollar and percentage on a public profile carries a clickable primary source and a confidence level (verified / inferred / unverified).
- Light-touch logo + website capture: a one-field upload/paste for sites lacking a confirmed mark or homepage (Cherbourg, Ceduna have null logo+site; Napranum, Mampu, Central Australia, Ngurratjuta have weak/placeholder marks). Where a site has no standalone site, default cleanly to the lead org's confirmed page rather than a generic gov index — most records already had to do this manually.
- Community-controlled 'people' workflow with cultural-safety gating: sites add named leaders themselves, with a Sorry Business / deceased-person suppression toggle and a 'name from community voice' default. 9+ sites have empty or thin people arrays specifically because naming was withheld for cultural safety — give them a safe, self-determined way to add names rather than leaving them blank.
- A network/relationship graph editor so sites can assert their own links (lead org, backbone, JRNA membership, state network like JRSA/SRWA, sibling sites). Several records had to disambiguate confusable entities (three 'Kurdiji' bodies; Ebenezer vs Old Ways New Ways in Balga; Ngurratjuta vs Mampu-Maninjaku) — a structured link layer prevents the platform repeating those merges.
- An 'evaluation readiness' track aimed at the late-2026 national-framework evaluations: a shared theory-of-change / baseline-data template and a metrics dictionary, so the ~20 establishment-stage sites capture comparable baselines NOW and aren't starting cold when evaluation funding arrives.
- A news/update feed each site can post to (most non-flagship sites have only 1-4 government announcement items and no self-published news), reducing dependence on NIT/government press releases as the sole public record.
- Surface the per-site federal grant amount: every record could only find the national $79M/$109M envelope, never the site allocation. JusticeHub can pull awarded values from GrantConnect / communitygrants.gov.au (which repeatedly returned 403 to ad-hoc fetches) via a proper data pipeline and attach the real figure to each site.
- Targeted outreach support for the thin-data and remote sites (Napranum, Ngurratjuta, Cowra, Ceduna, Mampu-Maninjaku, Central Australia) — pre-filled profile drafts the site can correct in one pass, lowering the capacity cost of being represented well.

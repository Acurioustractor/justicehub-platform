## 2. Revenue Model Analysis

### Verdict: WELL-DESIGNED BUT REQUIRES VIGILANT GOVERNANCE

**Stream 1: State Government Licenses** ($400K/year by Year 3)
- **Model**: QLD/NSW/VIC/WA/SA/NT/ACT/TAS subscribe to ALMA intelligence ($25-75K/year)
- **Community Benefit**: 30% revenue flows to Community Controlled sources
- **RISK**: Government uses intelligence to target communities, not support them
- **Mitigation Needed**: Ethical review board with community veto power

**Stream 2: Corporate Sponsorships** ($500K/year)
- **Model**: Companies sponsor intervention types (Education/Employment, Cultural Connection)
- **Community Benefit**: 60% flows directly to communities running programs
- **Example**: Atlassian sponsors Education/Employment → $60K to 12 programs
- **Assessment**: REGENERATIVE - Money flows to communities doing the work

**Stream 3: Research Partnerships** ($200K/year)
- **Model**: Universities license ALMA data with Indigenous governance oversight
- **Community Benefit**: 50% to Indigenous Advisory Board, 30% to community co-authors
- **Assessment**: Strong - Community co-authorship prevents extraction

**Stream 4: Story Licensing** ($50K+/year)
- **Model**: Media licenses storytellers' narratives
- **Community Benefit**: 50% to storyteller, 30% to platform, 20% to community org
- **Book publishers**: 100% to storytellers
- **Assessment**: EXCEPTIONAL - Storytellers own copyright from day one

**Stream 5: NETWORK Tier Free Access** ($100K value/year)
- **Model**: 20 community organizations get free platform access worth $40-50K each
- **Funded by**: Government + corporate revenue
- **Assessment**: Levels the playing field - Indigenous orgs get tech infrastructure

**Total Projected Revenue**:
- Year 1: $250K
- Year 2: $600K
- Year 3: $1.2M
- Year 5: $1.5M+ (self-sustaining)

**Community Benefit Flow**: 30-60% depending on stream → **$360K-$900K/year by Year 5**

## 3. Strategic Gaps Identified

### GAP 1: No Clear 10-Year Justice System Metrics ⚠️

**What's Stated**: "JusticeHub will be community-owned, internationally expanded"

**What's Missing**: "Australian youth justice will be..."

**Critical Question**: If JusticeHub succeeds, what changes in the actual justice system?

**Possible Metrics** (not currently stated):
- 75%+ youth justice encounters resolved in community (vs 25% today)?
- Zero detention for youth under 16?
- Indigenous incarceration rate parity with non-Indigenous?
- 50% reduction in recidivism nationally?

**Risk**: JusticeHub could become a successful platform supporting an unjust system.

**Recommendation**: Define clear justice system outcomes within 6 months.

---

### GAP 2: ALMA Signals Missing Community Voice ⚠️

**Current Signals**:
- System Pressure: Remand rates, detention length, staff turnover
- Community Capability: Indigenous governance, workforce stability, cultural continuity
- Intervention Health: Program continuity, staff burnout
- Trajectory: Re-entry patterns, school reconnection

**What's Missing**:
1. **Youth voice** - "Do youth feel heard by the system?"
2. **Family economic recovery** - Cultural continuity tracked but not economic outcome
3. **Indigenous parity** - Remand rates tracked but not disparity analysis
4. **Community power** - Decision-making authority in interventions

**Risk**: Framework could show "system is working" while Indigenous communities remain disproportionately harmed.

**Recommendation**: Add signals for youth satisfaction, Indigenous parity ratio, economic mobility, community decision-making power.

---

### GAP 3: No Ethical Review Process for Partnerships ⚠️

**Current State**: Revenue model well-designed but no governance structure for deal review

**Risk**:
- Government uses ALMA intelligence for surveillance
- Corporations use "partnership" for greenwashing
- Citation fees become hidden tax on community fundraising

**Recommendation**: Establish Indigenous Advisory Board with:
- Quarterly review of partnerships
- Veto power on government licenses
- Audit of revenue flows to communities
- Community opt-out mechanism

## Final Verdict

JusticeHub is **infrastructure designed to transfer to community ownership**. That's exceptionally rare.

Most platforms become extractive despite good intentions. JusticeHub has built-in safeguards:
- Revenue flows to communities (not just platform)
- Indigenous governance seats (structural, not advisory)
- Exit strategy (community trust by Year 5)
- Ethical constraints (enforced in code, not policy)

**But**: Without clear 10-year justice system metrics, JusticeHub could become a very successful platform supporting an unjust system.

**Recommendation**: Protect the community ownership intention fiercely. Add community voice signals to ALMA. Establish ethical review immediately. Then fund aggressively.

This isn't a typical nonprofit. It's infrastructure for a just future. Build it right.

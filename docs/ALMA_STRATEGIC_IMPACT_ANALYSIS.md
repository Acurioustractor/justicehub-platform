## Part 2: What ALMA Adds (The Intelligence Layer)

### ALMA's Role in JusticeHub Ecosystem

**ALMA = Adaptive Learning for Meaningful Accountability**

It's not just a database of interventions - it's an **intelligence system** that:

1. **Identifies what works** (and for whom, in what context)
2. **Surfaces community authority** (who controls programs, not just who funds them)
3. **Flags harmful approaches** (e.g., detention-focused interventions disguised as "support")
4. **Matches interventions to contexts** (what works in remote NT won't work in urban Sydney)
5. **Tracks evidence** (not just anecdotes, but measurable outcomes + lived experience stories)

### The 5-Signal Portfolio Framework

From the [12-week plan](file:///Users/benknight/.claude/plans/pure-exploring-marshmallow.md):

| Signal | Weight | What It Measures |
|--------|--------|------------------|
| **Community Authority** | 30% (HIGHEST) | Who owns/controls the program? Aboriginal-led > Community-led > Mainstream NGO > Government |
| Evidence Strength | 25% | Quality of outcome data (RCT > Evaluation > Promising > Unknown) |
| Harm Risk | 20% | Red flags (detention focus, punitive, no community input) |
| Implementation Capability | 15% | Can this be replicated? Sustainable funding? |
| Option Value | 10% | Learning potential, innovation, fills gaps |

**Critical Insight**: Community Authority is weighted HIGHEST. This isn't "nice to have" - it's the primary signal ALMA uses to rank interventions.

- **Current narrative**: "We're the experts in youth services"
- **ALMA narrative**: "Your Community Authority score is low. Oochiumpa's holistic model is more effective."
- **Threat**: Funders redirect grants to Aboriginal-led programs

**3. Academic Researchers**
- **Current narrative**: "We need RCTs to prove what works"
- **ALMA narrative**: "Oochiumpa's program evaluation (n=21, 6 months) shows 95% reduction. That's stronger evidence than your 3-year RCT with 60% re-offending."
- **Threat**: Community-generated evidence challenges academic gatekeeping

**4. Detention Industry**
- **Current narrative**: "Detention is necessary for public safety"
- **ALMA narrative**: "Oochiumpa diverted 18 of 19 young people from Operation Luna list. Detention is a failure by comparison."
- **Threat**: Existential - if community alternatives scale, detention contracts disappear

### The Backlash to Expect

**Predictable Responses**:
- **"Sample size too small"** - Oochiumpa (n=21) isn't "rigorous" like government programs (n=1000+)
  - **Counter**: Government programs have 40% re-offending. Oochiumpa has 5%. Sample size doesn't matter when outcomes are clear.

- **"Not scalable"** - Oochiumpa is "culturally specific" to Central Australia
  - **Counter**: ALMA found 5-10 similar programs across NT. It IS being replicated, just not by government.

- **"Conflicts of interest"** - Oochiumpa benefits financially from Intelligence Pack sales
  - **Counter**: Government departments have $XXX million budgets to protect. Who has the bigger conflict?

- **"Cherry-picking data"** - ALMA only highlights programs that work
  - **Counter**: ALMA flags High Harm Risk programs too. We're transparent about methodology.

**Why This Matters**:
If ALMA becomes influential (funders use it, policy makers cite it, youth choose services based on it), we're directly threatening **billions of dollars in detention/mainstream youth justice funding**.

That's not a bug - that's the point.

# LinkedIn post draft, ~210 words

Last Tuesday, fifty-five judges and magistrates sat on Country at Atnarpa, on Eastern Arrernte land east of Mparntwe.

They were welcomed by Elders. They walked the homestead. They held six postcards that fit in a chamber pocket, and they made a decision under the same constraint a sentencing officer is given every day: limited time, incomplete information, real consequences.

The day was hosted by Kristy Bloomfield and Tanya Turner of Oonchiumpa. The bench was asked to read one sentence before they boarded the flight:

*"Our young people are just collateral in a bigger issue. The issue doesn't sit with them."*

Read that sentence once and the courtroom stops being neutral.

Six voices were on the postcards. Kristy and Tanya. Jackquann and Nigel, both fourteen, both told plainly what would help, and neither asked for punishment. Laquisha, sixteen, who answered *what gets young people in trouble* in one word: oppression. Xavier, who built Stretch Beds from recycled plastic and ran home with his first pair of shoes.

The bench is back in chambers this week, postcards in hand.

The work has begun. The full piece, written for Empathy Ledger and surfaced on JusticeHub, is here:

With Kristy Bloomfield, Tanya Turner, and the team at Oonchiumpa.

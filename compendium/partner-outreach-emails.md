**Subject:** CONTAINED launches in Mount Druitt — partnership with Mounty Yarns

Hi team,

We're bringing CONTAINED to Mount Druitt in April — and we'd like Mounty Yarns to be our founding tour partner.

**What CONTAINED is:** One shipping container, three rooms, thirty minutes. Visitors experience the reality of youth detention (Room 1), a proven therapeutic alternative from Spain (Room 2), and the community-led organisations already doing the work (Room 3).

**What we're asking:**

1. **Host the container** at a Mounty Yarns venue or nominated site in Western Sydney for 2–4 days

4. **Share the social kit** — we'll provide branded assets, suggested copy, and a shareable link at `/contained/tour/social` that your community can forward

**What we bring:**

- Professional documentation of the event
- A social media toolkit with your branding alongside ours

- Empathy Ledger captures community stories with full consent protocols

**The numbers that frame it:**

- Australia spends **$1.55M per year** per detained young person (Productivity Commission ROGS 2024-25)

- Community alternatives cost **$75/day** with **3% reoffending** and **88% restorative justice success**

This isn't a lecture — it's an experience. And Mount Druitt is the right place to start.

Can we set up a call this week to walk through the logistics?

Warm regards,

## 3. Perth — UWA School of Social Sciences (May 2026)

**Status:** Exploring
**Ask:** Host on campus + academic collaboration
**To:** UWA School of Social Sciences

---

**Subject:** CONTAINED at UWA — immersive advocacy meets research

Hi,

We're exploring bringing CONTAINED to the University of Western Australia in May 2026, and the School of Social Sciences is the natural partner.

**What CONTAINED is:** A 30-minute immersive experience inside one shipping container. Three rooms take visitors through the reality of youth detention, a proven therapeutic model from Spain (Diagrama Foundation), and the community-led alternatives already operating in Australia.

**What we're proposing:**

1. **Host on campus** for 3–5 days — ideally a high-traffic location (main campus quad, social sciences courtyard, or similar). The container is self-contained — we manage setup, facilitation, and pack-down
2. **Academic collaboration** — connect CONTAINED with your research programs. JusticeHub's evidence engine (ALMA) indexes 876 verified interventions with full provenance chains. We'd welcome a research partnership exploring visitor impact, attitude change, or evidence communication effectiveness
3. **Student engagement** — invite social work, criminology, and Indigenous studies students to experience the container and contribute to Room 3 design. Community voices from WA shape this room
4. **Nominate WA decision-makers** — 5 names from state government, justice services, or media who should walk through the container. Our nominations platform handles the public pressure

**Why UWA:**

Western Australia has some of the highest rates of Indigenous youth incarceration in the country. The gap between what the evidence says works and what the system funds is enormous. CONTAINED makes that gap visceral — and JusticeHub makes the evidence accessible.

**The evidence base:**

- **$1.55M/year** per detained youth vs **$75/day** for community alternatives
- **84% reoffending** from detention vs **3%** from community programs
- **527+ organisations** indexed on ALMA with evidence ratings from Proven to Indigenous-led
- **73% success rate** at Diagrama (Spain) — the therapeutic model in Room 2

**What we need from UWA:**

- A confirmed venue and dates in May
- An academic contact to co-design the research component
- Support promoting to students and faculty

We'll handle everything else — build, transport, facilitation, documentation, and media.

Would a 30-minute call work to explore this further?

## 4. Tennant Creek — Indigenous Community Engagement (June 2026)

**Status:** Exploring
**Ask:** Community-controlled partnership + cultural authority
**To:** Community leaders (to be identified through existing relationships)

---

**Subject:** CONTAINED in Tennant Creek — community-controlled from the start

Hi,

We're exploring whether CONTAINED should come to Tennant Creek in June 2026. But unlike our other tour stops, this one only happens if the community wants it and controls it.

**What CONTAINED is:** A 30-minute experience inside one shipping container. Three rooms — the reality of youth detention, an international therapeutic alternative, and the organisations already doing the work. Every number is sourced. Every story is consented. Every Room 3 is designed by the local community.

**What community-controlled means here:**

1. **You decide if it happens** — if the timing isn't right, or the approach doesn't fit, we don't come. No pressure, no agenda beyond what serves the community
2. **Room 3 is entirely yours** — your programs, your people, your story. We provide the space and the platform. You decide what goes in it
3. **Cultural safety protocol** — our Empathy Ledger platform has built-in consent boundaries. No story is captured, shared, or published without explicit permission. Indigenous-led authority outranks institutional prestige in everything we do
4. **Local facilitation** — we want community members to facilitate the experience, not outsiders. We'll fund facilitator training and compensation

**What we bring:**

- Full funding for the Tennant Creek stop — container transport, setup, community engagement ($75,000 budgeted for this stop specifically)
- Professional documentation, but only what the community approves for release
- JusticeHub's evidence engine featuring local and regional programs
- Connection to the national tour — Tennant Creek's story joins Mount Druitt, Adelaide, and Perth in a national narrative about what works

**Why we're reaching out now:**

Indigenous young people are **23x overrepresented** in youth detention. The evidence for community-led alternatives is overwhelming — **$75/day** vs **$4,250/day**, **3% reoffending** vs **84%**. But the communities doing the best work are the least visible and least funded. CONTAINED exists to change that.

**The ask right now is small:** a conversation. Who should we be talking to? What would need to be true for this to work? What should we absolutely not do?

We'd welcome a yarn at whatever pace works.

Ben Knight
JusticeHub / CONTAINED

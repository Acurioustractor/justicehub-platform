# CONTAINED Research Brief

Date: March 12, 2026

## Why this matters

This brief turns the external research into two practical lanes:

- a cleaner public campaign narrative for `CONTAINED` and `JusticeHub`
- a safer, more useful agent strategy for `ALMA`

## Research Signals

### 1. Karpathy's autoresearch pattern

Source:

- https://github.com/karpathy/autoresearch

What matters:

- The core loop is simple: propose, run, evaluate, keep or revert.
- The setup is intentionally narrow: one mutable file, one metric, fixed time budget.
- The human sets intent in `program.md`; the agent iterates inside bounded constraints.

Implication for JusticeHub:

- Do not build a general-purpose swarm first.
- Start with tightly-scoped agent jobs, single metrics, and reversible outputs.
- The right first targets are evidence discovery, evidence linking, and campaign ops recommendations.

### 2. Hyperspace's compound learning stack

Source:

- https://github.com/hyperspaceai/agi

What matters:

- The collaboration model is `GossipSub -> CRDT leaderboard -> GitHub archive`.
- The search domain uses a ranking metric and compounds learning across nodes.
- The repo positions the network as a live research archive, not just a model endpoint.

Implication for JusticeHub:

- The useful pattern is distributed learning and visible audit trails.
- The wrong move would be importing generic search metrics like `NDCG@10` as-is.
- JusticeHub needs its own metric stack: trust, freshness, source quality, community authority, human acceptance, and consent safety.

### 3. FrameWorks messaging guidance

Sources:

- https://www.frameworksinstitute.org/framing-fundamentals/
- https://www.frameworksinstitute.org/articles/lead-with-the-idea-of-dignity-explain-the-frame-episode-1/
- https://www.frameworksinstitute.org/resources/quick-start-guide-to-framing-csa-prevention/

What matters:

- Lead with values, not raw conflict.
- Framing changes what people notice, understand, and support.
- Dignity is a strong opening value when talking about injustice.
- Do not front-load stark statistics without narrative context.
- Do not use “yes, but” language that accidentally repeats punishment logic.

Implication for JusticeHub:

- Open with dignity, community, and the possibility of change.
- Use the numbers after the audience understands the system and the alternative.
- Avoid messages that start and end inside detention.
- The campaign should make the affirmative case for community-led alternatives, not just rebut punishment.

### 4. Beautiful Trouble organizing guidance

Sources:

- https://beautifultrouble.org/training
- https://beautifultrouble.org/dispatches/wf0xf2ytroijdjdo5sgkr4fiw0o5hn
- https://beautifultrouble.org/dispatches/may-day-is-every-day

What matters:

- Build a strategic escalation plan, not isolated actions.
- Tailor action design and messaging to audience and target.
- Work across multiple points of intervention, not just one decision-maker.
- Create many points of entry so more people can join at different risk levels.

Implication for JusticeHub:

- The campaign should not rely only on event attendance.
- Every page needs a distinct role in the escalation ladder: witness, nominate, back, share, host, fund, document.
- The public story and the backend evidence system should reinforce each other.

## Recommended Message Architecture

- `CONTAINED` shows what youth detention feels like.
- `JusticeHub` shows what works instead.
- `Empathy Ledger` protects consent and community authority.
- `ALMA` keeps the evidence base live.

## Recommended Rollout Shape

### Public lane

- Lead with: dignity, truth, community-led alternatives.
- Then show: one shipping container, three rooms, thirty minutes.
- Then show: evidence, funding misalignment, and where the public can act today.

### Agent lane

- Agent 1: discovery
  Find programs, evaluations, hearings, budget changes, and partner signals.
- Agent 2: linking
  Connect claims to evidence and organisations to proof.
- Agent 3: ranking
  Prioritise trustworthy, fresh, community-accountable results.
- Agent 4: campaign ops
  Watch nominations, shares, media, and host readiness; suggest next pushes.

## Guardrails

- No automated scoring of lived experience submissions without human review.
- No optimisation toward clicks or controversy.
- Indigenous-led and community-led authority must be explicit ranking features.
- All public-facing agent outputs need provenance and review.

## The practical standard

If a feature does not make the public story clearer, the evidence chain stronger, or the action path easier, it is noise.

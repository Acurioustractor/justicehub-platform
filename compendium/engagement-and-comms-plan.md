## 3. Personal Email Structure (The Ben Knight Format)

Your best emails share a pattern. Keep using it:

```
Subject: [Specific, not salesy — "CONTAINED and [Their Org]" or "Something you should see"]

Hi [First name],

[1 sentence: why I'm writing to YOU specifically]

[2-3 sentences: what CONTAINED is — container, three rooms, thirty minutes]

[1-2 sentences: how YOUR work connects to this]

[The specific ask — ONE thing, crystal clear]

Ben
```

**Rules:**
- No "Dear" or "Kind regards" — first name, sign off with just "Ben"
- Never more than 150 words
- ONE ask per email. Not three. One.
- The ask is always a conversation, not a commitment: "Can we get on a call?" not "Will you fund this?"
- Include a stat ONLY if it serves the ask (cost comparison for funders, evidence for researchers)
- No links in the first email. Links come in the follow-up.
- Write like you talk. No marketing language. No "excited to share."

**Follow-up sequence (if no reply):**
- Day 5: Forward the original with "Hi [name], just floating this back up. No rush." (7 words)
- Day 12: New angle — share a piece of content (stat tile, photo, article) with "Thought you'd find this interesting."
- Day 20: Last touch — "I know you're busy. If the timing's wrong, no stress. But if you want to see what we're building: justicehub.org.au/contained"
- Then stop. Three touches max. Respect > persistence.

## 7. Supporting Champions

People who respond become your distribution network. Here's what to give them:

### The Champion Kit
When someone says yes (to anything — hosting, funding, sharing, nominating):

1. **Immediate:** Thank them personally. Not a template. A real reply within 2 hours.
2. **Within 24 hours:** Send them the social sharing kit link (`/contained/tour/social`)
3. **Within 48 hours:** Send them a one-page brief customized to their ask:
   - Funders get: budget breakdown, impact projections, what their money buys
   - Hosts get: logistics plan, container specs, what we handle vs what they handle
   - Amplifiers get: 3 ready-to-post social tiles + suggested copy + the stat that hits hardest for their audience
   - Nominators get: confirmation their nominee is in the system + what happens next
4. **Weekly:** Update them on progress. Brief. "4 new nominations this week. Adelaide confirmed. Here's the latest photo from the build."

### Make Champions Look Good
The secret to sustained support: make them the hero in their own network.

- Tag them in social posts when relevant
- Quote their support publicly (with permission)
- Send them the content BEFORE you publish so they can share simultaneously
- Give them the exclusive before the broadcast — "You're seeing this before anyone else"

## 8. Brand Discipline in Comms

Every piece of communication must pass these checks:

| Check | Rule |
|-------|------|
| Photos | REAL only. Never AI-generated photorealistic. Check against flagged list in brand guide. |
| Colors | `#0A0A0A` (text), `#F5F0E8` (backgrounds), `#DC2626` (CTAs only), `#059669` (success) |
| Language | "System-impacted young people" not "offenders". "Community authority" not "at-risk." |
| Stats | Every number has a named source. No rounding up. No "approximately." |
| Tone | Direct. Evidence-based. Community-centered. Never charity. Never trauma tourism. |
| CTA | Every piece ends with ONE of: Experience it / Nominate / Back the tour |
| Photography credit | "Photo: Empathy Ledger" or specific storyteller credit |

### What NOT to say
- "Excited to announce" — say "This is happening"
- "We believe" — say "The evidence shows"
- "At-risk youth" — say "young people affected by the system"
- "Our mission" — say "The work"
- "Make a difference" — say what the specific difference is

## 9. Content Flywheel: How Stories Generate More Stories

### The Cycle
```
1. CONTAINED tour stop happens
2. Visitors react → capture reactions (form, video, quotes)
3. Decision-makers walk through → capture their response
4. Local org fills Room 3 → document their story
5. Photographer captures everything (EL, consent-first)
6. Content team produces:
   - 3 social posts
   - 1 newsletter update
   - 1 partner spotlight
   - 1 stat update (new nominations, visits)
7. Published content draws new nominations + registrations
8. New interest feeds the next tour stop
```

### Content Types That Compound
| Type | Example | Shelf Life | Reuse |
|------|---------|------------|-------|
| Decision-maker quote | "I didn't know it cost $4,250 a day" — [Minister Name] | Forever | Social, email, media kit |
| Before/after reaction | Visitor facial expression walking in vs walking out | 2 weeks | Social, website |
| Room 3 org spotlight | "This is what PCYC does for $75/day" | 6 months | Website, funder packs |
| Data update | "47 decision-makers have now been nominated" | 1 week | Social, email subject lines |
| Real photo | Community gathering at tour stop | Forever | Everything |

### What Triggers What
- **A funder says yes** → LinkedIn post celebrating (tags them) → their network sees → new funder inquiries
- **A politician walks through** → Photo + quote (with permission) → media release → pressure on peers
- **A young person shares their story** (consented, EL protocols) → Most powerful content you'll ever have → handle with extreme care
- **You hit a milestone** (50 nominations, $25K raised, 4th city confirmed) → Stat tile → social → email update

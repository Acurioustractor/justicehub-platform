## 1. Core Line

**One container. Three rooms. The cure already exists.**

The reality of youth detention, the evidence for change, and the community-led alternatives already doing the work.

Secondary line: *CONTAINED shows what youth detention feels like. JusticeHub shows what works instead.*

Legacy line (still valid for print): *One shipping container. Three rooms. Thirty minutes.*

## 2. Color Palette

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Primary Black** | `#0A0A0A` | Body text, headings, primary backgrounds |
| **Off-White** | `#F5F0E8` | Page backgrounds, card surfaces — warmer than pure white |
| **Urgent Red** | `#DC2626` | CTAs, critical data callouts, accent moments. One intense moment is stronger than five. |
| **Emerald** | `#059669` | Success metrics, positive outcomes, community-led indicators |

**Rules:**
- Red is reserved for CTAs and critical statistics only. Never decorative.
- Off-white (`#F5F0E8`) is the default page background, not `#FFFFFF`.
- Black backgrounds use `#0A0A0A` for warmth, not `#000000`.
- Body text on light backgrounds: `#0A0A0A`. On dark backgrounds: `#F5F0E8`.
- One color accent per section. Restraint over saturation.

## 3. Typography

### Display — Space Grotesk
- Headings: **Bold (700)**, letter-spacing `-0.05em` (tight tracking)
- Hero headlines: 48–72px
- Section headlines: 32–40px
- Sub-headlines: 24–28px

### Data & Labels — IBM Plex Mono
- Statistics, data labels, captions: **Regular (400)** or **Medium (500)**
- Data callouts: 14–18px
- Captions and meta: 12–14px

### Hierarchy Rules
- Maximize contrast between display weight (heavy) and label weight (light/regular)
- Slightly tighter tracking on large type, open tracking on small caps and tiny labels
- Never use pure gray for body text — calibrate to palette warmth

## 4. Brand Voice

From the JusticeHub identity:

- **Community authority comes first** — lived-experience advisors shape direction. Community organisations control their data.
- **Evidence is story, not surveillance** — research is indexed without profiling individuals. Stories carry context.
- **Centre agency, not trauma** — lead with what works, not what's broken.
- **System-impacted, not offenders** — never describe young people as "offenders."
- **Justice infrastructure, not justice tech** — we build infrastructure communities can own.
- **Build for handover** — open-source, designed for community ownership.

### Tone
- Direct. Evidence-backed. Urgent but not sensational.
- Statistics earn their place — every number cited has a source.
- The ask is never abstract. The ask is to back what works.

### Email & Outreach Voice (LOCKED)
Ben's outreach emails are **human, warm, vulnerable, and real**. Not formal. Not corporate. Not pitchy.

**Rules:**
- First name, casual greeting ("Hey", "Quick one", not "Dear")
- Conversational — write like you're texting a friend who happens to run a foundation
- Be honest about what you don't have figured out yet — vulnerability is the brand
- Show momentum with specifics, not polish (real places, real numbers, real names)
- The ask is always soft — "keen to grab a coffee", "jump on a call", not "request a meeting"
- Acknowledge their contribution personally — what it specifically unlocked, not generic gratitude
- End human — "Hope to talk soon", "Let us know", not "Kind regards"
- OK to say you're screwing things up, overwhelmed, building while flying — that's the truth and people respect it
- Never use: "I hope this email finds you well", "Kind regards", "I'm reaching out to", "I wanted to touch base"
- Always use: real talk, specific details, genuine asks, Ben's actual voice

## 9. Language Rules

### Always use
- **System-impacted young people** (not "offenders," "juvenile delinquents," or "at-risk youth")
- **Community-led alternatives** (not "diversion programs")
- **Justice infrastructure** (not "justice tech" or "justice platform")
- **What works** — lead with solutions, not problems
- **Evidence-based** — every claim backed by a named source

### Never use
- "Offenders" or "juvenile offenders"
- "At-risk" without qualifying who defined the risk
- "Tough on crime" framing, even to critique it (don't amplify)
- AI-generated photorealistic images of real people or places
- Sensationalised language about young people's experiences

### Framing
- **The ask is concrete**: back what works, not abstract reform
- **Centre community authority**: the communities most affected deserve the biggest say
- **Evidence is overwhelming**: the debate is settled, the question is political will
- **This isn't a lecture, it's an experience**: CONTAINED is felt, not taught

### Messaging Framework (updated 2026-03-18)

**This is NOT about selling tickets. It's about getting events funded and running.**

CTAs and language should always frame CONTAINED as a movement that needs backing:

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| Get Tickets / Book Now | **Back This** / **Back This Stop** |
| Register | **Support This Stop** |
| Now Showing | **Funded** |
| Coming Soon | **Seeking Support** |
| An Immersive Exhibition Experience | **Help Make This Happen** |
| See It For Yourself | **Help Us Get There** |
| What You Can Do | **How You Can Help** |

**Three action pillars:**
1. **Fund a Stop** — each tour stop costs ~$15K to stage
2. **Spread the Word** — share evidence, contact your MP
3. **Host the Container** — offer a venue, festival, or community space

## 10. The Three Rooms (updated 2026-03-18)

| Room | Title | Duration | What It Is |
|------|-------|----------|------------|
| **Room 1** | Current Reality | 10 min | Sensory recreation of what a 15-year-old experiences inside an Australian youth detention centre. Concrete, strip lighting, isolation. |
| **Room 2** | What Works | 10 min | Based on our visit to Diagrama Foundation's youth justice centres in Spain. Education, therapy, connection instead of concrete. 13.6% recidivism, 1:1 staffing, weekly family contact. |
| **Room 3** | The Organisations Already Doing It | 10 min | Celebrates the grassroots orgs doing the work — Indigenous-led and community orgs that aren't being valued, reported on, or funded the way they should be. Changes each tour stop to feature the local host organisation. |

**Room 2 key stats:** Recidivism 13.6% · Staff ratio 1:1 · Social return €5.64 per €1 · Weekly family contact

## 12. Visual Style

### Bauhaus / Constructivist Influence
- High contrast, angular compositions
- Clip-paths and diagonal cuts for visual energy
- Brutalist grid with generous whitespace
- Red wedge accent (constructivist heritage) — used sparingly

### Layout Principles
- Information on surfaces, not boxed in cards
- Asymmetric scale contrast (large headline next to small muted data)
- Fewer elements, highly refined
- White space is a feature, not wasted space
- One intense color moment per section

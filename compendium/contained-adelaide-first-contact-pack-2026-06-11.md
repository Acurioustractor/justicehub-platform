## The master note

**Subject options:**
- Thirty minutes inside the question
- A container is coming to Adelaide. You should walk through it.
- What support had to exist before the crisis?

**Body:**

In the last week of June, a shipping container stands in public space in Adelaide, on Kaurna Yarta. Young people and supported crews build it at the start of the week. From Wednesday 24 to Friday 26, people walk through it. You walk through alone. Thirty minutes. Phone and shoes stay at the door.

Inside are three rooms. The first is a cell like the ones we send children into. The second is what Spain does instead: a desk, pencils, a daily schedule that looks like a life. The third is what South Australia could do next, the organisations already doing the work, and one question waiting at the exit: who needs to walk through this before they make the next decision?

CONTAINED runs beside the Reintegration Puzzle Conference (Wednesday 24 and Thursday 25 June) with the Justice Reform Initiative. Mount Druitt was the first stop. Adelaide is the second. The container keeps moving.

The exact site is announced soon. Book, and the address comes to you the moment it is confirmed.

**[audience opener and ask go here, see below]**

**How to take part:**

1. **Walk through it.** Book one 30-minute slot, Wednesday 24 to Friday 26 June: justicehub.com.au/contained/register
2. **Nominate someone.** Who needs to walk through this before they make the next decision? Put their name down: justicehub.com.au/contained#nominate
3. **Bring one person** who would never normally come to something like this. They are who it was built for.
4. **Put your organisation in the room.** Room 3 carries a wall of South Australian organisations doing the work. One line, your sign-off, your door open to whoever walks through.
5. **Open a paid role for a young person.** Greeter and host shifts are paid, supervised, and supported, arranged through youth organisations.
6. **Back the next stop.** The container moves after Adelaide: justicehub.com.au/contained/invest

Everything starts here: justicehub.com.au/contained/adelaide

Stories inside CONTAINED are held under Empathy Ledger consent protocols. Nothing enters the experience without the storyteller's say-so.

CONTAINED is a JusticeHub campaign by A Curious Tractor with the Justice Reform Initiative, running alongside the Reintegration Puzzle Conference.

## Audience openers

Swap one of these in above the take-part block. Each pairs with an existing template for the fuller personal version; this pack is the shorter, forwardable form.

### 1. Supporters and warm public

You have followed this work for a while, which is why this lands with you first. The container you heard about is real, it is in Adelaide this month, and there is a slot with your name on it. Booking is free and takes a minute. The favour we ask is the third item below: bring someone.

*Ask: book + bring one person. Channel: personal now; `contained-adelaide-invite` stream after 16 June. Fuller version: template 7.*

### 2. Youth organisations

You hold the relationships, and they stay yours. Our question is whether any young people in your network want paid, supported work as greeters and hosts during the Adelaide week, with breaks, a support person on site, and a clear right to say no at any point. The opening question we ask each young person is simple: why do you want to do it, and what would you like to earn? Nothing about any young person enters this experience without their say-so, on their terms, with their people around them.

*Ask: nominate young people for paid roles + talk consent pathways. Channel: personal to org leaders only, never bulk, never directly to young people. Fuller version: template 6.*

### 3. Universities and researchers

CONTAINED puts the evidence in a room you can walk through, and then it leaves town. What stays behind is whatever Adelaide chose to look at while it was here. That is where you come in: host a student or practitioner walkthrough block, help make the South Australian evidence visible and source-checkable, or lend people to visitor flow and journal transcription during the week. Endorsement is not the ask. The ask is practical: help the evidence outlast the container.

*Ask: a practical role, not attendance. Channel: direct partnership conversation (no template exists yet; this opener is it).*

### 4. Services and practitioners

You spend your days holding young people the system keeps failing. CONTAINED was built so the people above you in the decision chain can feel, for thirty minutes, what you already know. Book a walkthrough, and bring one colleague who holds budget or caseload decisions. If your organisation belongs on the Room 3 wall, say the word: one line, your sign-off, printed only when you approve it.

*Ask: book + bring a budget-holder + wall card. Channel: personal now; `youth-justice-brief` stream after 16 June. Fuller version: template 4.*

### 5. Funders and philanthropy

Mount Druitt was the proof. Adelaide is the pattern: young people build it, decision-makers walk it, and JusticeHub turns every walkthrough into evidence and action. Walk through it yourself, then give us twenty minutes on what the next stop needs. The machine exists. Backing extends it.

*Ask: walk through + 20-minute conversation. Channel: personal only until the post-week brief. Fuller version: template 2.*

### 6. Decision-makers (courts, MPs, policy)

This one is thirty minutes, private, and unfilmed. Nobody photographs you, nothing is asked of you inside, and the evidence behind every wall is citable. The question the container asks is simple: what support had to exist before the crisis? I think you are one of the people who can act on the answer. We will hold a priority slot on Wednesday or Thursday morning; reply and it is yours.

*Ask: one priority slot, Wed 24 or Thu 25. Channel: personal only, from the relationship owner, never bulk. Fuller version: template 1.*

### 7. Conference delegates (Reintegration Puzzle)

You will spend Wednesday and Thursday at Reintegration Puzzle talking about what happens after the system. CONTAINED, a short walk away, asks what should have happened before it. Slots fit around sessions. Carry one question back into the room: what support had to exist before the crisis?

*Ask: book around sessions. Channel: BLOCKED for direct send (no delegate list yet); JRI carries this inside the conference until the import lands. Fuller version: template 5.*

# CONTAINED

## One container. Three rooms. The cure already exists.

A shipping container stands in public space in Adelaide, on Kaurna Yarta. Young people built it. You walk through it alone. Thirty minutes. Phone and shoes stay at the door.

**Room one is the current reality.** A cell. Shampoo, noodles, a bench. This is where Australia sends children. It costs $1.55 million per child per year (Productivity Commission ROGS 2024-25), and 84% reoffend within two years of release (AIHW Youth Justice 2023-24). Indigenous children are 23.1 times more likely to be locked up than their non-Indigenous peers (ROGS 2024-25).

**Room two is what works.** In Spain, the Diagrama Foundation runs centres built on family contact, education and one-to-one care. Reoffending falls when relationships hold. A desk. Pencils. A teddy bear. A daily schedule that looks like a life. Another system is not a theory. It is running, now, and Australians have walked its corridors.

**Room three is what next.** South Australian organisations are already doing this work. JusticeHub has catalogued 981 alternatives across the country (ALMA Evidence Database) and the funding tells the story: roughly $15 goes to punishment for every $1 that reaches what works (calculated from ROGS 2024-25). You leave with one action chosen, not a pamphlet.

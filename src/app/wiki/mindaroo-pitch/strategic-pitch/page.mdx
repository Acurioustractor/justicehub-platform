### The Crisis

<Callout type="error" title="Overrepresentation">
  Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander young people are **17 times more likely** to be in detention than non-Indigenous young people, despite being only 6% of the youth population.
</Callout>

**What's Not Working:**
- **Best-practice programs hidden in communities** - Knowledge not shared across sector
- **Indigenous knowledge extracted without compensation** - Perpetual colonial dynamics
- **Practitioners spend 30-40% of time writing unsuccessful grants** - Exhausting, demoralizing
- **Philanthropic dependency cycles** - No pathway to self-sufficiency
- **Data about communities controlled by others** - Lack of Indigenous data sovereignty

### The Opportunity

### What This Investment Does

<Callout type="success" title="Scaling What Works">
  <strong>This budget doesn't build the platform from scratch - it scales what's already working.</strong>
</Callout>

**The BASE Scenario ($10.5M) delivers:**
- **CORE/NETWORK model**: 5 ‚Üí 15 ‚Üí 29 communities total (CORE communities paid $180K/year, NETWORK communities receive $40-50K value through free support)
- **Year 1**: 5 CORE communities
- **Year 2**: 7 CORE + 8 NETWORK communities (15 total)
- **Year 3**: 9 CORE + 20 NETWORK communities (29 total)
- **Community Connectors**: 2 ‚Üí 6 ‚Üí 10 FTE delivering NETWORK support and coordination
- **Indigenous participation** embedded throughout all project activities and governance
- **15-20 community members** trained in platform management, content creation, technical skills
- **21 training modules** for practitioner development
- **Community-owned ACCO** by Year 5
- **Revenue generation** pathway to 50-70% self-sustaining by Year 7

### Potential Warm Community Partners

<Callout type="info" title="CORE/NETWORK Model">
  <strong>CORE communities</strong> receive $180K/year and governance rights. <strong>NETWORK communities</strong> receive $40-50K value through free Community Connector support.
</Callout>

**CORE Community Candidates (7 identified, 5 ‚Üí 7 ‚Üí 9 funded across 3 years):**

1. **Mparntwe (Alice Springs, NT)** - Oonchiumpa Consultancy - Cultural camps, 50+ youth annually
2. **Minjerribah (North Stradbroke Island, QLD)** - MMEIC (Minjerribah Moorgumpin Elders-in-Council) - 20+ years experience, 2017 NAIDOC Award
3. **Barranbinya (Bourke, NSW)** - Bourke Aboriginal Community - Pioneering justice reinvestment
4. **Yaama (Moree, NSW)** - Moree Aboriginal Community - Cross-sector integration model
5. **Bwgcolman (Palm Island, QLD)** - Palm Island Community Company - Bwgcolman Youth Program, $235K QLD funding
6. **Darug Country (Mount Druitt, NSW)** - Mounty Yarns - Youth-led storytelling and advocacy
7. **Kalkadoon Country (Mount Isa, QLD)** - BG Fit - Community fitness and wellbeing programs

**NETWORK Communities (8 ‚Üí 20 across Years 2-3):**
- Identified through grassroots outreach and practitioner referrals
- Smaller programs ready to benefit from free support
- No payment required, reducing barrier to entry
- Community Connectors deliver training, content creation assistance, platform access

**Each CORE community receives $180K annually for:**
- Organization infrastructure ($50K)
- Knowledge holder payments ($50K at consultant rates)
- Content creation ($30K)
- Community gatherings ($20K)
- Equipment & technology ($15K)
- Cultural protocols ($15K)

## Next Steps

<Callout type="success" title="5-Phase Process to Launch">
  <strong>Timeline:</strong> 4-5 months from first meeting to Year 1 begins
</Callout>

**1. Deep Dive Meeting (2 hours)**
- Walk through full proposal
- Clarify questions and concerns
- Discuss Mindaroo's priorities and fit

**2. Community Consultation (1 month)**
- Visit 2-3 partner communities with Mindaroo
- Hear directly from community leaders
- See programs in action

**3. Co-Design Workshop (1 day)**
- Co-design Year 1 workplan with all 5 Year 1 CORE communities
- Establish governance and decision-making processes
- Finalize community partnership agreements

**4. Due Diligence (2 weeks)**
- Financial review and audit
- Legal structure and compliance
- Reference checks with community partners
- Value-for-money assessment

**5. Formal Agreement (1 month)**
- Detailed 3-year workplan
- Grant agreement and milestones
- Reporting and evaluation framework
- Decision gates and review points

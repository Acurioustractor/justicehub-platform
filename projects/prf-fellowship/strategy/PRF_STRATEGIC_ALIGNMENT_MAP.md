## PRF Principles â†’ Fellowship Practice

**How Fellowship Enables Agency:**
- Communities choose which stories to share
- Communities edit AI-extracted content before publication
- Communities decide when to participate in revenue-sharing
- Communities control their data and narratives
- Research serves community goals, not just academic outputs

**Evidence of Agency:**
- Ownership frameworks (communities OWN stories, not just tell them)
- Consent processes (ongoing, not one-time)
- Economic participation (communities benefit when stories create value)
- Co-research (communities shape questions, interpret findings)

---

### Relationships

**PRF Definition:** *[Long-term partnerships, trust, reciprocity]*

**How Fellowship Prioritizes Relationships:**
- Long-term partnerships with host communities (not extractive research)
- Reciprocal: JusticeHub provides infrastructure, communities provide knowledge, BOTH benefit
- Trust-building: Communities approve all content before publication
- Relational ethics: If research doesn't serve community, we don't do it

**Not Transactional:**
- Not: "We'll study you and publish about you"
- Instead: "We'll co-research together and you'll own the infrastructure"

### Reciprocity

**PRF Definition:** *[Mutual benefit, giving and receiving, fair exchange]*

**How Fellowship Embodies Reciprocity:**
- **Economic:** Revenue-sharing when stories create value
- **Knowledge:** Communities gain research capacity; researcher gains community wisdom
- **Impact:** Research serves community goals (policy change) not just academic outputs
- **Infrastructure:** Communities gain platform they continue to own after fellowship ends

**Evidence of Reciprocity:**
- Communities compensated for participation
- Economic model redistributes value
- Research outputs serve community advocacy
- Platform ownership remains with communities

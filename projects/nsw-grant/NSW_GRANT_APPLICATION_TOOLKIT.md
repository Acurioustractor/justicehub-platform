## Section 1: Program Objectives - Evidence-Based Responses

### **Objective 1: "Deliver holistic responses that address underlying needs"**

#### ❌ Standard Application Language:
> "We will provide mentoring and cultural activities to address the underlying needs of at-risk youth."

#### ✅ JusticeHub-Informed Language:

> **Our program recognizes that "underlying needs" are actually system failures:**
>
> **Evidence from JusticeHub Research:**
> - NSW Youth Koori Court evaluation (BOCSAR 2022) demonstrates that addressing underlying factors through Elder-led action plans reduces custody by 84% and custodial sentences by 40%[^1]
> - Queensland data shows 75% of youth reoffend within 2 weeks of release when programs fail to address housing, family stability, education re-engagement, and mental health simultaneously[^2]
> - Lowitja Institute scoping review (2024) of 15 Indigenous diversion programs found that programs scored HIGH for cultural responsiveness only when they addressed housing, family, education, health, AND cultural connection together—not in isolation[^3]
>
> **Our holistic approach will:**
> - Map EVERY system interaction each young person experiences (child protection, housing, education, police, health)
> - Document how government agencies create barriers (school suspensions, housing denials, inadequate mental health services)
> - Build evidence for integrated funding across departments, not siloed youth justice programs
> - Position young people and families as experts on what underlying needs actually are
>
> **This is holistic analysis of systems, not just holistic support for individuals.**

[^1]: BOCSAR NSW Youth Koori Court Evaluation (2022) - [Documented in JusticeHub Research Library](https://justicehub.au/research)
[^2]: Queensland Audit Office Youth Justice Report (2024) - [Documented in JusticeHub Research Library](https://justicehub.au/research)
[^3]: Lowitja Institute Cultural Responsiveness Review (2024) - [Documented in JusticeHub Research Library](https://justicehub.au/research)

### **Objective 2: "Culturally responsive initiatives with long-term resilience"**

#### ❌ Standard Application Language:
> "We will incorporate Aboriginal culture into our program through consultation with Elders."

#### ✅ JusticeHub-Informed Language:

> **Cultural responsiveness requires Aboriginal governance, not Aboriginal consultation.**
>
> **Evidence from JusticeHub Research:**
> - NSW Youth Koori Court gives Elders decision-making authority over action plans—resulting in 40% reduction in custodial sentences. Elders are not consultants; they are decision-makers[^1]
> - Lowitja Institute review found that only 10 of 15 Indigenous diversion programs scored HIGH for cultural responsiveness. The defining factor: Indigenous-led governance with Elders having genuine authority, not advisory roles[^3]
> - International models documented in JusticeHub (Diagrama Spain, Missouri Model) show that therapeutic approaches combined with cultural authority reduce recidivism to 8-14% compared to 75-96% in traditional systems[^4]
>
> **Our program structure:**
> - Elders Circle governs program design, not staff
> - Aboriginal workers make case management decisions, non-Aboriginal workers support under their direction
> - Connection to Country programs designed and led by Traditional Owners
> - Cultural safety audited by community, not by us
> - All data owned by Aboriginal organization, not shared with NSW without community approval
>
> **We do not seek funding to "deliver culturally responsive services." We seek funding for Aboriginal community governance of justice responses.**

[^4]: International Best Practice Models documented in JusticeHub Research Library - Diagrama Foundation Spain (13.6% recidivism), Missouri Model (8% recidivism)

### **Objective 3: "Empower young people and families to achieve change"**

#### ❌ Standard Application Language:
> "We will empower young people through skill-building programs and positive role models."

#### ✅ JusticeHub-Informed Language:

> **Young people don't need empowerment programs. They need systems to stop failing them.**
>
> **Evidence from JusticeHub Research:**
> - Queensland data: 75% of youth reoffend within 2 weeks because they return to the same housing instability, family crisis, school exclusion, and mental health gaps that caused justice involvement[^2]
> - JusticeHub documentation of 454 organizations and 521 programs shows that individual "empowerment" programs fail when structural barriers remain: child protection removal, homelessness, education exclusion, racist policing[^5]
> - BackTrack Youth Works (documented in JusticeHub) achieves 87% employment transition not through "skill-building" but through addressing transport, housing, family support, and employer racism simultaneously[^6]
>
> **Our approach to empowerment:**
> - Young people will document their experiences with police, courts, child protection, schools, housing—positioning themselves as experts on system reform
> - Families will co-design interventions, not receive services
> - Young people will present findings to NSW Parliament, judiciary, and police—shifting narrative from "at-risk youth" to "systems at risk of failing young people"
> - JusticeHub platform deployment will enable young people to share stories and connect to youth-led advocacy nationally
>
> **Empowerment means changing the systems that disempower, not teaching young people to cope with oppression.**

### **Target 11: Closing the Gap - 30% Reduction in Aboriginal Youth Detention by 2031**

#### ❌ Standard Application Language:
> "Our program will contribute to Closing the Gap by reducing Aboriginal youth offending."

#### ✅ JusticeHub-Informed Language:

> **Target 11 will fail if NSW continues funding "innovative programs" instead of scaling proven models.**
>
> **Evidence from JusticeHub Research:**
> - NSW already has the solution: Youth Koori Court reduces Aboriginal youth custody by 84% and custodial sentences by 40% (BOCSAR 2022)[^1]
> - Current NSW approach: Youth Koori Court is optional, dependent on police referral and judicial discretion
> - To meet Target 11, NSW needs mandatory diversion to Youth Koori Court for all Aboriginal young people—not more pilot programs
>
> **What JusticeHub evidence shows works:**
> - **Justice Reinvestment:** Bourke Maranguka achieved 46% reduction in domestic violence through community-led, place-based, long-term investment (10+ years, not 2 years)[^7]
> - **International models:** Finland has only 4 youth in custody nationally; Scotland uses therapeutic interventions for under-16s; both raised age of criminal responsibility to 12-14[^8]
> - **Aboriginal data sovereignty:** Communities that own and control data about what works can hold governments accountable for meeting Target 11
>
> **Our program's contribution to Target 11:**
> - Document barriers preventing referral to Youth Koori Court
> - Build community navigator roles to increase Aboriginal young people accessing Koori Court
> - Generate evidence for policy change: mandatory diversion, not optional programs
> - Connect to Closing the Gap Target 1 (early childhood) by mapping child protection pipeline to justice system
> - Assert Aboriginal data sovereignty so communities can report on Target 11 progress independently of government
>
> **Target 11 requires systems change, not service delivery. This grant will build evidence to demand that change.**

## Section 6: Risk Management - Naming the Real Risks

### **Assessment Criterion: "Risk mitigation strategies"**

#### ❌ Standard Response:
> "Risks include staff turnover, low youth engagement, and insufficient referrals. We will mitigate through strong supervision, engaging activities, and partnership with police."

#### ✅ JusticeHub-Informed Response:

> **The real risks are:**
>
> **Risk 1: Co-optation**
> - **What it is:** Government absorbs community activists into service delivery, reducing advocacy capacity
> - **Evidence:** Documented in Aboriginal health and justice sectors nationally
> - **Mitigation:**
>   - Separate program delivery from advocacy functions
>   - Young people trained to publicly challenge NSW if needed
>   - Aboriginal data sovereignty ensures government can't control narrative
>
> **Risk 2: Extraction of Aboriginal Knowledge**
> - **What it is:** Community generates valuable data, government uses it without community control
> - **Evidence:** Common in government-funded research and programs
> - **Mitigation:**
>   - Aboriginal data sovereignty clause in funding agreement
>   - Community owns intellectual property and methodology
>   - No data shared without community approval
>
> **Risk 3: Short-Term Funding, Long-Term Expectations**
> - **What it is:** 2 years is insufficient to address intergenerational trauma and systemic racism
> - **Evidence:** International models need 10+ years; QLD's $134M over 5 years still showed 75% reoffending[^2][^4]
> - **Mitigation:**
>   - Build permanent infrastructure, not temporary programs
>   - Revenue generation strategy for sustainability
>   - Use grant to advocate for 10+ year Justice Reinvestment investment
>
> **Risk 4: Government Declares "Success" Prematurely**
> - **What it is:** NSW uses small improvements (5-10% reduction) to claim Target 11 progress while avoiding systemic change
> - **Evidence:** Common in Closing the Gap reporting
> - **Mitigation:**
>   - Benchmark against international standards (Diagrama: 13.6% recidivism) not NSW's low bar
>   - Publicly report community-controlled data
>   - Position young people to hold government accountable
>
> **Risk 5: Police Fail to Refer Young People**
> - **What it is:** Program depends on police referrals, but police resist diversion
> - **Evidence:** Youth Koori Court is optional; police discretion limits access
> - **Mitigation:**
>   - Document every barrier to referral
>   - Build evidence for mandatory diversion policy
>   - Use community-owned data to publicly challenge police
>
> **Risk 6: Funders Expect "Deficit Narrative" About Young People**
> - **What it is:** Government wants reporting on youth "problems," not system failures
> - **Evidence:** Standard grant reporting focuses on individual pathology
> - **Mitigation:**
>   - Report on system failures alongside individual outcomes
>   - Train young people to tell their stories challenging dominant narrative
>   - Assert Aboriginal data sovereignty over how data is interpreted
>
> **The biggest risk is replicating what's already failing. Our mitigation is refusing to do that.**

## Section 7: Outcome Measures - Measuring Systems Change, Not Just Individual Change

### **What NSW Wants You to Measure:**
- Number of young people served
- Reduction in offending
- Improved school attendance
- Positive feedback from participants

### **What JusticeHub Evidence Says to Measure:**

#### ✅ JusticeHub-Informed Outcomes Framework:

**Outcome Domain 1: System Accountability**
- **Measure:** Number of documented system failures per young person (child protection, housing, education, mental health, police)
- **Benchmark:** Queensland data shows 75% reoffend because systems fail; our program should document an average of X system failures per young person
- **Reporting:** Annual report to NSW Parliament on how government agencies fail young people
- **Why:** Shifts focus from "fixing youth" to "fixing systems"

**Outcome Domain 2: Aboriginal Governance**
- **Measure:** Number of decisions made by Elders vs decisions made by staff
- **Benchmark:** NSW Youth Koori Court model—Elders have authority over action plans
- **Reporting:** Annual cultural safety audit by community, not external evaluator
- **Why:** Ensures cultural responsiveness is real, not performative

**Outcome Domain 3: Community Power**
- **Measure:** Number of young people presenting to NSW Parliament, police, judiciary about system reform
- **Benchmark:** At least 10 young people by Year 2 positioned as experts on justice reform
- **Reporting:** Media coverage, policy submissions, public advocacy documented
- **Why:** Shifts young people from "participants" to "advocates"

**Outcome Domain 4: Data Sovereignty**
- **Measure:** Percentage of data owned and controlled by Aboriginal organization (should be 100%)
- **Benchmark:** Community can use data for advocacy without government permission
- **Reporting:** Annual report on how data was used for community purposes
- **Why:** Prevents extraction of Aboriginal knowledge

**Outcome Domain 5: Infrastructure Sustainability**
- **Measure:** Revenue generated from training, consulting, licensing by Year 2
- **Benchmark:** 10-15% self-sufficiency by Year 2, 35-40% by Year 5 (Mindaroo model[^9])
- **Reporting:** Financial sustainability plan with path to community ownership
- **Why:** Reduces dependence on government grants

**Outcome Domain 6: Individual Wellbeing (Yes, We'll Measure This Too)**
- **Measure:** Young people's self-reported connection to culture, family, education, employment
- **Benchmark:** NSW Youth Koori Court shows 84% less custody when underlying factors addressed[^1]
- **Reporting:** Youth-led storytelling on JusticeHub platform (qualitative + quantitative)
- **Why:** Individual wellbeing matters, but only when systems change

| 70% reduction in offending | X young people diverted to Youth Koori Court (proven 84% custody reduction) |

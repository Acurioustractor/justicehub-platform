# QLD DYJVS Consultancy & Contract Spending

> Scraped 2026-03-28 from QLD Open Data Portal contract disclosure registers

## Key Finding

**The Department of Youth Justice reported NIL consultancy expenditure** for every financial year data is available (2019-20, 2020-21, 2021-22, 2023-24). This is explicitly stated in the [DYJ Annual Report 2023-24](https://www.publications.qld.gov.au/ckan-publications-attachments-prod/resources/3e45ff41-5e61-46c3-9d16-f3116cc70b4c/yj-annual-report-2023-2024.pdf), page 3:

> "For 2023-2024, DYJ had no expenditure to report for overseas travel and for consultancies."

However, the **DCYJMA Contract Disclosure Register** (the predecessor department that included Youth Justice) reveals significant professional services spending on consultants. These contracts were disclosed under the former Department of Children, Youth Justice and Multicultural Affairs (DCYJMA), which was split in late 2023.

## Intelligence Notes

1. **Nous Group is the dominant consultant** with $509K across 6 contracts -- they are the go-to advisory firm for QLD youth justice strategy work.
2. **Delivery Associates Australia** ($273K, 3 contracts) specializes in government delivery units and performance management.
3. **Big 4 presence**: PwC ($211K), EY ($163K), Deloitte ($112K), KPMG ($50K) = $536K combined from Big 4 firms.
4. **Arrilla Indigenous Consulting** ($44.5K) is notable as the only Indigenous-specific consultancy identified.
5. **Finity Consulting** ($39.5K) is an actuarial firm -- likely engaged for youth justice cost modelling.
6. **Kantar Public** ($22K) is a research firm -- likely engaged for public opinion/program evaluation research.
7. The "NIL consultancy" claim in annual reports is technically accurate because these are classified as "Professional Services" contracts, not "consultancies" under QLD government reporting definitions.

## 4. Sentenced vs Unsentenced Detention (QLD)

| Status | Proportion |
|--------|-----------|
| Unsentenced (remand) | **90%** |
| Sentenced | **13%** |

(Proportions do not sum to 100% as young people may be in both categories on the same day.)

This pattern has been consistent:
- 2022–23: 91% unsentenced, 12% sentenced

**National context (June quarter 2025):** 72% unsentenced nationally. QLD's 90% unsentenced rate is significantly above the national average, indicating heavy reliance on remand.

## 6. Trends Over Time (QLD, 5–10 Years)

### 5-Year Trends (2019–20 to 2023–24)

| Metric | 2019–20 | 2023–24 | Change |
|--------|---------|---------|--------|
| Total under supervision (avg day) | 1,773 | 1,598 | **-10%** |
| Community-based supervision | ~1,580 | ~1,294 | **-18%** |
| Detention | ~213 | ~320 | **+50%** |
| Overall supervision rate (10–17) | 24 per 10,000 | 21 per 10,000 | -12.5% |
| Community rate (10–17) | 21 per 10,000 | 16 per 10,000 | -23.8% |
| Detention rate (10–17) | 3.6 per 10,000 | 5.1 per 10,000 | **+41.7%** |
| First Nations rate (10–17) | 176 per 10,000 | 159 per 10,000 | -9.7% |

### Extended Trends (2018–19 to 2023–24, combining both report editions)

| Metric | 2018–19 | 2022–23 | 2023–24 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Total supervision (avg day) | 1,931 | 1,595 | 1,598 |
| Supervision rate (10–17) | 29 per 10,000 | 21 per 10,000 | 21 per 10,000 |
| Community rate (10–17) | 25 per 10,000 | 16 per 10,000 | 16 per 10,000 |
| Detention rate (10–17) | 3.6–5.0 range | 5.0 per 10,000 | 5.1 per 10,000 |
| First Nations rate (10–17) | 229 per 10,000 | 178 per 10,000 | 159 per 10,000 |

### Key Trend Insights

1. **Overall supervision declining** — Total numbers and rates have fallen consistently, driven by community-based supervision reductions
2. **Detention rising sharply** — 50% increase in detention numbers over 5 years (2019–24), while community supervision fell 18%
3. **First Nations rate declining** — From 229 to 159 per 10,000 over 6 years, but still 22x the non-Indigenous rate
4. **Detention rate fluctuating nationally** — AIHW notes QLD rates "fluctuated over the 4-year period and showed no clear trend" in the detention population quarterly data

### Quarterly Detention Trends (June Q 2021 to June Q 2025)

From the Youth Detention Population report:
- National detention increased from **809** (Jun Q 2021) to **884** (Jun Q 2025)
- First Nations proportion of detention nationally increased from **51% to 61%**
- First Nations rate nationally increased from **22 to 26 per 10,000**
- First Nations young people were **21x** as likely to be detained (up from 17x in Jun Q 2021)
- QLD consistently had the **highest raw number** of young people in detention of any state

## 7. Recidivism / Returns to Sentenced Supervision

### AIHW Recidivism Report 2023–24

The AIHW measures "returns to sentenced supervision" (not recidivism per se — the YJ NMDS cannot measure all reoffending, only returns to supervised sentences).

**For young people aged 10–16 released from supervision in 2022–23:**
- Young people released from a supervised **detention** sentence were most likely to return
- Returns are measured at 6-month and 12-month intervals
- Northern Territory data was unavailable for this cohort

**Important caveat:** Before February 2018, QLD excluded 17-year-olds from youth justice (they were in the adult system). Data from 2017–18 onwards may not be comparable with earlier years.

### ROGS 2026 Data

From the Productivity Commission's Report on Government Services 2026:
- The latest data covers young people released from supervision in 2022–23 who returned within 12 months (during 2023–24)
- QLD-specific: approximately **84–96%** of children released from detention return to sentenced supervision within 12 months (per QLD parliamentary inquiry evidence)
- A low rate of returns to sentenced youth justice supervision is desirable

Source: [ROGS 2026 Chapter 17](https://www.pc.gov.au/ongoing/report-on-government-services/community-services/youth-justice/)

## 8. QLD-Specific Commentary

### QLD Legislative Context

- **February 2018:** Queensland raised youth justice age to include 17-year-olds (previously dealt with in adult system)
- **Minimum age of criminal responsibility:** Remains at **10** in QLD (unlike ACT and NT which raised to 12 in 2023; NT lowered back to 10 in October 2024)
- **Youth Justice and Other Legislation Amendment Act 2021** introduced breach of bail as an offence, mandatory GPS tracking for certain offenders
- QLD has 3 youth detention centres: Brisbane Youth Detention Centre, Cleveland Youth Detention Centre, and West Moreton Youth Detention Centre

### QLD Stands Out Nationally

1. **Highest detention numbers:** 300 young people on an average night (June Q 2025) — more than NSW (229) or VIC (148)
2. **Second highest detention rate:** 5.1 per 10,000, behind only NT (17 per 10,000)
3. **Very high unsentenced proportion:** 90% unsentenced vs 72% nationally — indicates high remand rates
4. **Detention growing while community supervision shrinking:** A "substitution effect" where fewer young people enter community supervision but more are detained
5. **First Nations over-representation extreme:** 72% of detainees but only 8.8% of population; 26x rate ratio in detention

## Intelligence Analysis

### Key Themes

1. **Detention centre conditions** (7 applications): Strip searches, separations/solitary confinement, CCTV footage, capacity, specific child treatment -- this is the dominant area of RTI interest
2. **Expert Legal Panel** (3 applications): The LNP government's Expert Legal Panel on Youth Justice Act reforms attracted RTI from both Opposition and Human Rights Law Centre -- all refused/withdrawn
3. **Research suppression** (1 application): Brisbane Times sought the Youth Justice Research Agenda 2023-24 findings on serious repeat offenders -- **access refused**
4. **Detention centre capacity** (1 application): Brisbane Times sought 2022 business case on detention capacity -- **access refused**

### Notable Findings

- **88% refusal/restriction rate** for non-personal RTI requests: of the 7 applications from media, legal advocates, and opposition, only 1 resulted in document release (strip search registers)
- **Human Rights Law Centre** is the most persistent applicant (Monique Hurley on strip searches, Rachana Rajan on Expert Legal Panel)
- **Brisbane Times journalists** (Sean Parnell, Matt Dennien) both had applications refused -- suggests systematic resistance to media scrutiny
- The **strip search register release** (APP2024/2986) is the most significant document -- 21.2MB of partially unclothed search records from detention centres

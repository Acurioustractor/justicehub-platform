## Methodology

### Data collection

JusticeHub aggregates data from 35 government and philanthropic sources through a combination of:

- **Government open data portals**: structured data from AusTender (federal procurement), Queensland Government Information Portal (QGIP), NSW eTendering, and state-level open data platforms
- **Statistical publications**: Productivity Commission Report on Government Services (ROGS), Australian Institute of Health and Welfare (AIHW), Bureau of Crime Statistics and Research (BOCSAR)
- **Organisation registers**: Australian Charities and Not-for-profits Commission (ACNC), Office of the Registrar of Indigenous Corporations (ORIC)
- **Performance audits and oversight reports**: Queensland Audit Office, NSW Auditor-General, Commonwealth Auditor-General
- **Parliamentary records**: Hansard transcripts (federal and state), ministerial diary disclosures
- **Web scraping and structured extraction**: automated collection from government websites, with source URLs preserved for each record
- **Community verification**: program and organisation data verified through community-controlled organisations and the Empathy Ledger storytelling platform

### Evidence rating methodology

Interventions are rated on a five-level evidence scale:

1. **Proven**: Program has been evaluated using randomised controlled trials (RCTs) or quasi-experimental methods, with positive outcomes replicated across multiple sites or studies.
2. **Effective**: Program has undergone strong independent evaluation demonstrating positive outcomes for participants.
3. **Promising**: Program is community-endorsed with emerging evidence of positive outcomes, but has not yet been subject to rigorous independent evaluation.
4. **Indigenous-led**: Program is culturally grounded with community authority and governance. Evidence framework centres community-defined outcomes and cultural indicators alongside Western evaluation methods.
5. **Untested**: Program is at theory or pilot stage, without formal evaluation data.

### Organisation classification

Organisations are classified by control type based on:

- **Community-controlled**: Governed by an Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander board; registered with ORIC; or self-identified as Aboriginal community-controlled with verifiable governance structures.
- **Intermediary**: Non-Indigenous not-for-profit or for-profit organisations delivering services to Indigenous communities.
- **Government**: Commonwealth, state, or local government agencies or statutory bodies.

### Funding linkage

Funding records are linked to organisations using ABN matching and name-matching algorithms. Of 148,386 records, 45.1 per cent (66,958) are currently linked to identifiable recipient organisations. The 54.9 per cent unlinked rate is attributable to:

- State-level aggregate reporting (e.g. ROGS data, which reports by jurisdiction rather than by recipient)
- Inconsistent disclosure formats across jurisdictions
- Records where the recipient is described in text rather than identified by ABN or registered name

### Data currency

All data in this submission is current as of 31 March 2026. The JusticeHub platform is updated continuously through automated collection and community verification. Specific data currency by source:

- **ROGS**: 2024–25 edition (published January 2025)
- **BOCSAR**: Most recent available statistical series (accessed March 2026)
- **AIHW**: Most recent available statistical series (accessed March 2026)
- **AusTender**: Updated daily via API
- **QLD open data**: Updated as published by the Queensland Government
- **ACNC/ORIC**: Updated quarterly

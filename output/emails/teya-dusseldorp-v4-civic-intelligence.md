**The platform now tracks what government promises vs what government does.**

We built a civic intelligence layer that embeds and cross-references 6,436 chunks of Hansard debate, ministerial statements, and oversight body recommendations. It's live at justicehub.com.au/intelligence/civic.

What it shows:

- **75 charter commitments** tracked against delivery status. The pattern is stark — commitments made publicly, quietly abandoned.
- **56 oversight recommendations** from inspectors, ombudsmen, and auditors — mapped against whether government acted on them. Most: no.
- **A promise tracker** that shows the gap between political rhetoric and program funding in real time.

This matters for Dusseldorp's portfolio because it proves the structural pattern your funding decisions already respond to. You invest where government fails — 43% of your youth justice dollars go to ACCOs vs less than 1% of NSW government grants. The civic intelligence page now documents that failure with primary source evidence, not opinion.

**The NSW Sector Report is complete.**

40 sources. 154 mapped programs. The full picture: $327M/year, 34% detention surge since the bail laws, $0 in reoffending grants to ACCOs, 550:1 cost ratio. It documents what Dusseldorp has been demonstrating with its portfolio — that philanthropic funders are structurally better at directing money to community-controlled organisations.

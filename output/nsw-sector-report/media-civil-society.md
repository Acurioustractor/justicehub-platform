## Executive Summary

NSW is in the grip of a youth justice crisis driven by the March 2024 Bail and Crimes Amendment Bill. Youth detention has surged 34% since June 2023, with 234 young people in custody as of June 2025. Aboriginal children make up ~60% of the detention population despite being ~5% of NSW youth population. The bail laws -- extended for 3 more years in early 2025 -- have been condemned by virtually every legal, human rights, and Aboriginal organisation in the state. A NSW Legislative Council Select Committee on Youth Justice (established November 2025, reporting December 2026) and a Federal Senate Inquiry (interim report February 2025) are both examining the crisis. Against this backdrop, proven community-led alternatives like Maranguka (Bourke), Yuwaya Ngarra-li (Walgett), and the Youth Koori Court continue to demonstrate superior outcomes at a fraction of the cost.

## Community-Led Alternatives

### Maranguka Justice Reinvestment (Bourke)
- First Aboriginal-led place-based justice reinvestment in Australia
- Partners: Bourke Tribal Council, Just Reinvest NSW
- KPMG Impact Assessment (2017 data): 23% DV reduction, 31% Year 12 retention increase, 42% custody days reduction, 3.1M economic impact (5x operating costs)
- **Source:** https://www.justreinvest.org.au/community/bourke-maranguka/

### Yuwaya Ngarra-li / Two River Pathway to Change (Walgett)
- ACCO-led diversion model since 2018
- Partners: Dharriwaa Elders Group + UNSW
- Court appearances reduced to 7.8%; 83 outcomes since 2020; Youth Wellbeing Service est. 2024
- Featured in AHRC Help Way Earlier supplementary report
- **Source:** https://www.dharriwaaeldersgroup.org.au/index.php/yuwayangarrali/youth-justice

### Youth Koori Court
- Locations: Parramatta (2015), Surry Hills (2019), Dubbo (2023)
- 40% less likely to receive custody; no adverse reoffending impact
- 10th anniversary Feb 2025; 150+ young people supported
- **Source:** https://childrenscourt.nsw.gov.au/criminal/koori-court.html

### Mt Druitt Justice Reinvestment
- Aboriginal-controlled youth service; Mounty Yarns project
- Funded Jun 2024-Jun 2027

### Moree Justice Reinvestment

## Legislative Timeline (3 Major Bills)

### 1. Bail and Crimes Amendment Act 2024 (March 2024)
- New s22C bail test for 14-18 year olds on repeat serious offences
- "Performance crime" offence for filming crimes
- Fiercely opposed by legal sector

### 2. Bail Law Sunset Extension (May 2025)
- Extended to October 2026 (3-year extension)
- $6M+ bail extension support package
- ALS CEO Karly Warner: "These bail laws have put another generation of young children into an express training program for a life of crime."

### 3. Children (Criminal Proceedings) Amendment Bill 2025 (November 2025)
- Codified doli incapax into statute
- Strengthened prosecution rebuttal powers
- Expanded diversionary tools

## Political Positions (4 Camps)

### Labor (Premier Minns)
- Dual approach: tough bail + investment in diversion
- **Ruled out raising age of criminal responsibility**
- Framing: "community safety first, with evidence-based investment"

### Coalition (Henskens/Saunders)
- Wants harder measures: electronic monitoring, mandatory curfews
- Proposed expanding bail offences to all serious indictable crimes
- Bill voted down by Labor

### Greens (Boyd)
- Raise age of criminal responsibility to **16**
- Raise detention age to **18**
- Close all youth detention centres

### ALS / Legal Sector
- Fiercely opposed to bail crackdown
- ALS CEO Karly Warner: *"These bail laws have put another generation of young children into an express training program for a life of crime."*
- 500+ legal practitioners signed open letter against bail laws

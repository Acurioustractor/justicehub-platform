# NSW Procurement, Tenders & Transparency

**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Source:** Web research across buy.nsw, DCJ, GIPA, Audit Office

---

## 1. eTendering / buy.nsw

**Major transparency gap:** NSW procurement portal does NOT allow filtering by "youth justice" as a sub-category (QLD's contract disclosure register does).

### Sample Contracts Found
| Organisation | Value | Service |
|-------------|-------|---------|
| Lives Lived Well | $4M | Youth justice services |
| Youth on Track Riverina | $1.25M | Early intervention |
| Save the Children | $450K | Youth programs |
| Anglican Community Services | $611K | Youth services |

- Class 3 contracts ($5M+) published under GIPA Act
- No central searchable register equivalent to QLD Contract Disclosure

## 3. GIPA Disclosure Log

- Youth Justice records available from **1991** (CIMS incident reports, community files, detainee files)
- Log is **chronological and untagged by topic** — worse than QLD's categorised RTI log
- No easy way to determine refusal rates for YJ-specific requests

---

## 4. Auditor-General — Key Audits

### Audit 1: Reintegrating Young Offenders
- **<50%** working or studying on release
- Only **33%** in community activities post-release
- Inadequate transition planning

### Audit 2: First Nations Audit
- 5 strategies introduced but **NONE tracked spending or outcomes**
- **"Little to no impact"** on reoffending
- **56%+** of youth in custody are Indigenous (3.4% of population)
- Damning finding: strategies existed on paper but not in practice

---

## 5. Consultancy Spending

- YJ-specific consultancy spend is **NOT separately reported**
- Unlike QLD's explicit "NIL" disclosure (which masks $1.85M in "professional services")
- Buried in DCJ-wide aggregate financials
- **Transparency gap** — cannot determine how much advisory spending goes to youth justice

---

## 6. NSW vs QLD Transparency Comparison

| Dimension | NSW | QLD | Better |
|-----------|-----|-----|--------|
| Contract filtering by YJ | No | Yes (DYJVS filter) | QLD |
| Annual report YJ detail | Aggregate | Detailed | QLD |
| Consultancy disclosure | Not reported | "NIL" (misleading) | Tied (both bad) |
| GIPA/RTI categorisation | Chronological | By topic | QLD |
| YJ-specific spending breakdown | Via ROGS only | Via ROGS + contracts | QLD |
| Audit coverage | 2 relevant audits | QAO Report 15 | Tied |
| Parliamentary scrutiny | Select Committee (Dec 2026) | Senate + state | Tied |
| Cost per day data | $2,814 (ROGS) | $2,714 (ROGS) | Tied |
| Active inquiry | Yes (Nov 2025) | Yes (Senate Jun 2026) | Tied |

**QLD wins 5 of 9 transparency dimensions.** NSW is harder to audit.

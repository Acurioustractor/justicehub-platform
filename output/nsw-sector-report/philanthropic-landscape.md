# Australian Philanthropic Youth Justice Landscape

**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Source:** Web research + JusticeHub DB (PRF, Dusseldorp, Minderoo, NIAA records)

---

## Tier 1: Direct Youth Justice / Justice Reinvestment Funders

### Paul Ramsay Foundation
- **$53.1M invested in justice reinvestment** since 2021
- Just Futures program: $9M to 11 orgs (6 First Nations-led)
- JR portfolio: 30 records in DB, $174M tracked (incl. Commonwealth JR)
- Partner network: 71 orgs across Justice & Safety, First Nations, DFV, Employment
- Key recipients: Maranguka ($3.54M), Just Reinvest NSW ($3.54M), ALS NSW/ACT ($3.54M), Justice Reform Initiative ($3.54M), Mounty Yarns ($600K), The Hive Mt Druitt ($4.2M)
- **Focus:** Breaking cycles of disadvantage, justice reinvestment, place-based approaches

### Dusseldorp Forum
- Co-seeded Maranguka (Australia's first JR site) from 2013 with VFFF
- 21 grants in DB ($2M YIR 2025)
- Key: Mounty Yarns ($250K), Yoorrook Justice Commission ($100K), IndigiGrow ($50K)
- Focus: Youth, education, place-based, First Nations, climate, democratic renewal
- **Teya Dusseldorp** — active JusticeHub relationship (score 90)

### Tim Fairfax Family Foundation
- **$7.7M/year** total giving
- Direct YJ grants: Yiliyapinya and Gunawuna Jungai in QLD
- QLD-focused, Indigenous arts & culture, community development
- Geography: Primarily QLD/regional

### Vincent Fairfax Family Foundation (VFFF)
- Co-seeded Maranguka with Dusseldorp Forum (2013)
- Focus: Ethical leadership, responsible business, social cohesion
- Supported early justice reinvestment feasibility work

### NIAA (Government — Justice Reinvestment)
- **$69M National Justice Reinvestment Program** — 30 sites
- 26 of 30 sites funded as of 2025
- **$20M/year ongoing** from 2026-27
- $12.5M National Justice Reinvestment Unit
- Palm Island Community Company: $5.25M + $4.78M
- **Bridges government and philanthropy** — PRF portfolio review tracks Commonwealth JR alongside private funding

## Tier 2: PLACE Initiative (Collaborative Model)

### PLACE — National Centre for Place-Based Collaboration
- **$38.62M total** (philanthropic pool + government matching)
- $19.31M philanthropic in DB
- **Funders:** Dusseldorp Forum, Minderoo Foundation, Paul Ramsay Foundation, Ian Potter Foundation, Bryan Foundation
- Matching Commonwealth funding
- Focus: Place-based approaches to disadvantage — direct pipeline to justice reinvestment sites
- NSW-based

## Summary: The Philanthropic vs Government Investment Story

### Total Philanthropic Investment Mapped
| Funder | Amount | Direct JR? |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Paul Ramsay Foundation | $53.1M (JR only) | **Yes** |
| NIAA (govt JR program) | $69M + $20M/yr | **Yes** |
| PLACE Initiative | $38.62M | Indirect |
| Dusseldorp Forum | $2M+ | **Yes** |
| Minderoo | $30M (all programs) | Partial |
| Tim Fairfax | $7.7M/yr (all) | **Yes** |
| Other foundations | ~$5M+ | Mixed |
| **Total philanthropic (JR-specific)** | **~$125M+** | |

### The Contrast
- **Philanthropic funders:** $125M+ into justice reinvestment, community alternatives, ACCOs
- **QLD Government:** $536M/year — 57% on detention infrastructure
- **NSW Government:** $327M/year — bail law surge driving 34% detention increase

Philanthropy funds what works. Government funds what doesn't.

### Who Funds ACCOs?
- **PRF:** 6 of 11 Just Futures grantees are First Nations-led
- **NIAA:** Majority of recipients are ACCOs
- **Dusseldorp:** ~40% of grants to Indigenous-led orgs
- **Government (QLD):** 3.7% of top contract value to ACCOs (27:1 intermediary ratio)
- **Government (NSW):** 19:1 intermediary-to-ACCO ratio

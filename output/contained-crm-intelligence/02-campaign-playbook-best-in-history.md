## 1. Thesis: what kind of campaign CONTAINED must be to win

CONTAINED is not an awareness campaign with a clever object at the centre. It is a conversion machine wearing the body of an art installation. The container is the strongest emotional front door in Australian youth justice, one steel box, three rooms, thirty minutes, alone, phone and shoes at the door. But the door is not the point. The door is the ignition. Every great campaign in the research proved the same two things in sequence: a room can move a person more than a report ever will, and a moved person does nothing unless the room hands them the next thing to do before the feeling fades. Kony 2012 had the feeling and no structure, and nineteen thousand Sydney registrations became twenty-five people in a park. The Ice Bucket Challenge had the feeling and a biorepository underneath, and the spike funded a real drug. CONTAINED must be the second kind. So the thesis is a single architecture: the container moves the body, JusticeHub holds the evidence, the escalation ladder spends the movement, and Aboriginal-led community authority sits at the centre of all of it. Money flows inward, evidence flows outward, story stays sovereign at the centre. Win condition is not impressions or attendance. Win condition is rung-movement: how many people walked out and climbed from witness to nominate to stand to host, and how many decision-makers stood inside before their next decision about a young person. Build the catcher before you open the tap, keep the affected communities as authors and never as subjects, and the most powerful thirty minutes in the country becomes the most durable movement in it.

## 2. The Engine: witness, capture, nominate, share, commit

The research converges on one spine: at the threshold of leaving, the campaigns that changed outcomes did three things and the ones that failed skipped them. Hand over a single concrete action at peak emotion, capture the person so the relationship continues, and give them a way to carry the room to someone else. Here is that spine built into CONTAINED's exact mechanics, with the source campaign named for each adopted play.

### Step 0: Pre-assign the rung before the door opens

The losing move at peak emotion is a menu. Five asks produce a polite nod and paralysis. Every campaign that converted offered exactly one next thing: EJI said collect the soil from your county, ACLU Nebraska said cap isolation at fifteen days, Clouds Over Sidra said give now. So before any booked visitor arrives, their relationship owner assigns one rung, decided in the CRM, not improvised at the exit.

- A magistrate or MP gets nominate-and-cite: witness privately, then name who else needs to walk through before their next decision.
- A funder gets back-the-next-city: the $30K-a-stop frame, then a twenty-minute conversation at `/contained/invest`.
- A service org gets stand-with-it: get your organisation onto the next city's Room 3 wall.
- The warm public gets witness-and-bring-one: book, and bring one person who would never normally come.

The container is constant. The single ask is tailored. *(Source: "one named action, not a menu", EJI, ACLU Nebraska, Clouds Over Sidra; the per-audience single ask is already in CONTAINED's segment matrix.)*

### Step 1: The 30 minutes (witness): proximity is the product, protect it like the asset it is

Bryan Stevenson's whole thesis is one sentence: proximity is the key to our capacity to make a difference. The Legacy Museum does not argue you into a position, the descending floor IS the argument and you reach the conclusion in your own body. CONTAINED already holds this: Room 1 is a fifteen-year-old's reality, Room 2 is what works instead, Room 3 is the organisations already doing it. The non-negotiable is the rule that separates CONTAINED from every empathy-machine critique: never start and end inside detention, always arrive at the alternative. Room 1 humanises, Room 3 hands you toward the cure. Hold that line and the "you made me feel bad and left" critique becomes a credential. *(Source: proximity as strategy, EJI; "feel then act, never end in the cell", #cut50's legislation-plus-humanisation sequence.)*

The one thing the launch must not do is convert the container into a panel backdrop. The walk-through is the argument. Dumb Ways to Die won a lecture-averse young audience by refusing to lecture, the catchy song got in before the defences went up. The container is CONTAINED's catchy song. No slides inside.

### Step 2: The after-the-room ask (capture + nominate): convert at the threshold, in human hands

This is the single most important mechanic in the campaign and the research is unambiguous about why. UNICEF's Clouds Over Sidra roughly doubled face-to-face donations, one in six instead of one in twelve, because the ask happened with the headset still warm and a human standing there with an open envelope. Stanford's Becoming Homeless got 85% petition signatures because the petition was right there at the end. The conversion window is seconds, not days. Defer the ask to an email later and "later" becomes "never."

So the play: a trained young host stands at the container exit holding two physical objects, a printed nomination card and a single-question reflection card, and frames the locked door-opener question:

> "Who needs to walk through this before they make the next decision about a young person? Write their name."

An exit QR takes the visitor to `/contained` to complete the nomination digitally in ten seconds. The human at the threshold is the conversion engine. The post-experience email is the backup, never the front line, especially while `EMAIL_ENABLED` is unverified in prod. *(Source: "the ask lives at the threshold", Clouds Over Sidra's open envelope, Stanford's petition-at-the-exit.)*

### Step 3: The capture (the CRM is the house, the experience is the reason to capture)

Capture is the campaign. The experience is just the reason to capture. Kony 2012 failed because nineteen thousand registrations had a form behind them and nothing else, no path from registration to the twenty-five who showed. The Ice Bucket Challenge produced 1.2 million videos and only about 107,000 donors because most challengers performed belonging and never gave. The defence is structural: every walk-through is captured at the threshold so footfall becomes a follow-up path, not anonymous attendance, and the empty Adelaide booking funnel gets filled by hand this week off `roster.json`, never a tag query. An inbound lead going cold because nobody replied is the most expensive miss in the book, and fourteen "In conversation" contacts asked about the container and have not yet been told it is happening. *(Source: "capture is the campaign", Kony's empty funnel versus ALS's biorepository.)*

### Step 4: The take-home and the share (the nomination is the soil jar)

EJI's Community Remembrance Project is the masterclass in turning a visit into a job. The Montgomery visit is the ignition, but the soil jar dug in your own county and the marker installed in your own town are the ripple, more than 80 markers and soil from roughly 700 sites came from handing local people a tangible piece of the work to finish. CONTAINED's soil jar is the nomination made physical and trackable. The visitor leaves having named a real decision-maker, and the nightly nominee bridge turns that name into a real CRM record and a personal invite carrying the nominator's warrant softly: "someone who works alongside you asked us to make sure you were invited to walk through this before your next decision." The visitor's job is to get the person they named into the same room.

The share tile is the receipt of an embodied act, never a substitute for it. The Ice Bucket nomination is CONTAINED's exact mechanic pointed at power instead of friends, and the whole engine is the interpersonal warrant: the tile must read *a named person who respects you asked us to make sure you walked through this*, never *the campaign invites you*. And every tile points one rung up the ladder, it never closes the loop. *(Source: take-home that finishes the work, EJI soil jars; forced naming with the friend-warrant, Ice Bucket; "always end on the pledge", Dumb Ways to Die.)*

### Step 5: Close the proof loop (commit): "you did this"

charity:water solved the trust gap that sinks most giving by sending donors back GPS coordinates and a photo of the exact well their money built, with the line "you did this," and that proof loop is what turned one-time givers into believers who give again. CONTAINED produces charity:water's photo automatically, every walk-through is a located, dated, real event, built by young people in public space, and that is the image (real photos only, per brand rule). The play: when a nominator's decision-maker actually walks through, tell the nominator, *the person you named stood inside it on this date, here is what they saw.* When funders' backing fills the container, send them the real photo of real people inside it, "your backing put decision-makers inside it in Adelaide" beats any projection deck. This loop is currently broken because the nominee never becomes a tracked record, document the lightweight fix. *(Source: proof-of-impact loop, charity:water's "you did this.")*

### The ladder, named end to end

Witness → Nominate → Stand with it (back/share) → Host or partner (host/fund/document). Beautiful Trouble's ladder of engagement, run with one discipline: offer the next rung, never the summit. A first-time LinkedIn commenter is asked to witness, not to fund a $30K stop. A funder who has walked through is asked to fund, because they are already high on the ladder. No money ask before a walk-through or a real human exchange. Witness first, always. *(Source: ladder of engagement, match the rung to the person, never skip, Beautiful Trouble / the Commons Library.)*

## 3. Coalition and Organizing: the 78 become a self-propagating, Indigenous-led base

The 78 are not an audience to broadcast to. They are the first ring of a snowflake. A broadcast list of 78 is small. A structure where each of the 78 carries one relationship outward is a base of hundreds before Adelaide opens.

### Public narrative: order every touch self / us / now (Ganz)

Marshall Ganz's discipline is to lead with a person, then a we, then a choosable now, and never to open with the despair number, because the size of the problem produces despair, not action. CONTAINED already holds all three stories.

- **Story of self.** Ben's locked email voice is already a story of self, honest, specific, "here is what isn't figured out yet." It opens every personal touch to the 78.
- **Story of us.** The container is the story of us made physical. Room 3, the wall of SA organisations, is the "we" the visitor joins. When the 34 SA orgs see themselves on that wall, the wall says "you are us," which is why the consent calls are not admin, they are the story-of-us built one yes at a time.
- **Story of now.** "Doors open Tuesday 23 June" is the now. The nominate mechanic is the choice inside it. The SA cost frame lives once, inside the now, as the hinge and never the headline: $3,261 a day to hold one child in South Australia (ROGS 2024-25). *(Source: story of self/us/now, Marshall Ganz.)*

Concrete: rebuild the funder-23 invite and the inbound-14 reply on the self/us/now spine. Self (Ben's why, two sentences), us (Mount Druitt happened, Adelaide is stop two, the orgs on the wall, you belong in this), now (doors 23 June, your thirty minutes, the one question to carry in).

### The snowflake and the hub (Sunrise + Bernie)

The snowflake multiplies a tiny staff: every leader's job is to develop the leadership of others. For a two-person founder team this is the freeing lesson, Ben and Nic cannot personally carry every city. Name the snowflake explicitly. The first ring is the 7 Partner/committed plus the hottest of the 14 inbound. Each one gets a single carry-job tied to their world, not a generic "please share." Hannah March at the Justice Reform Initiative already is this, she unblocks an entire conference audience through one relationship. Find the other six. Each tour city is a hub: one method (three rooms, the question, the ladder), local autonomy (the city's own orgs on the wall), central spine (CONTAINED, JusticeHub, the consent protocol, the figures). Build the Adelaide hub-kit as you go so the tour is a structure, not a sequence of founder heroics. *(Source: snowflake leadership model and hub structure, Sunrise Movement.)*

### Relational organizing: nominate is the BERN app (Bernie 2020)

"Who needs to walk through this before their next decision" asks a supporter to name a specific decision-maker from their own world, exactly as the BERN app asked supporters to log the people in their lives. The nominator's relationship is the warrant. But a logged action that goes nowhere kills trust, so run the nominee bridge nightly through launch week, by hand for now, every nomination becoming a personal invite carrying the nominator's warrant. Never automate the touch to a nominee, relational means a human carries it every time. Hand the 78 a one-paragraph forwardable invite with their own name on it, so they make the ask into their own networks, CONTAINED amplifies by handing the ask outward, not by sending more itself. *(Source: relational organizing and the BERN app, Bernie 2020; kitchen-table "bring one person", Australian Marriage Equality.)*

### The bring-one multiplier (Marriage Equality)

The container is a better kitchen table than the kitchen table. The campaign that won the 2017 postal survey turned its base into messengers, each working their own network, with the line "I'm voting Yes, can I tell you why?" CONTAINED's version is one line on every personal invite and every booking confirmation: bring one person who would never normally come. The witness becomes a messenger at the moment of highest intent. And the clicktivist gap is real, a LinkedIn comment is not an organiser, so every warm commenter gets a same-day human reply and a concrete next rung, not a thank-you. *(Source: crowdsourced story and the one-to-one conversation, Australian Marriage Equality.)*

### Indigenous authority is structural, not decorative (Uluru + Maranguka + OCAP)

This is the spine, and it is the one rule that separates CONTAINED from Kony 2012. The moment CONTAINED looks like reformers saving Indigenous kids on camera, it has become the campaign that imploded in two weeks.

- **Room 3 is a community-governed dialogue, not a curated logo wall.** Before any SA org goes on the wall, hold a small SA-org gathering or one call each (the 34 Personal-outreach contacts) where they author their own panel in their own words. The ask is "help us hold this room," not "can we use your logo." The Uluru Statement earned its authority upstream, in who decided, before anyone read the words. This is the single act that converts the campaign's biggest risk (extraction) into its biggest strength (authentic authority), at the exact point the launch depends on it, because the 34 are the consent gate for Room 3.
- **The org is the messenger, the young person is never extracted.** When Aboriginal Legal Rights Movement, KWY, Nunkuwarrin Yunti walk through and say "this is real," the message becomes theirs, not CONTAINED's. The 34 are trusted witnesses, not promoters. Maranguka's lesson is authority, not participation: the community decided, it was not consulted. CONTAINED points at the work, it does not annex it. The instant the campaign implies it runs or ranks the orgs on the wall, it becomes the extractive thing it critiques.
- **OCAP and "nothing about us without us" as a binding layer.** Empathy Ledger is the consent, story and community-authority layer. A story enters CONTAINED only with consent, stays under the storyteller's control, and can be withdrawn. Run a "nothing about us without us" check on every public surface before it ships, alongside the seven Imagination Architect tests. Young people are paid co-authors who build and host the rooms, never props, consent ongoing and revocable. *(Source: authority from process, Uluru Statement; community owns the model, data and savings story, Maranguka; OCAP and "nothing about us without us", FNIGC / Global Indigenous Data Alliance.)*

## 4. Funding model: cultivation, ask, stewardship (charity:water proof loop, canon-safe)

The funder lane inverts the usual order. Most campaigns ask for money and then prove impact. CONTAINED lets the funder feel the impact first, in their body, in thirty minutes, then makes the ask small, specific, and already underway. The container is the cultivation event other organisations spend years trying to manufacture.

**The price list, held to two numbers.** Fund a stop: $30K (roughly the cost of detaining one child for about seven days at the SA rate of $3,261/day, ROGS 2024-25). Build a new container: $50 to 70K (CONTAINED Campaign Budget). That is the entire price list. Never promise "100% to the container," it feeds the overhead myth and it is not true, $30K buys the build, the paid young hosts, and safe delivery, and all of it is the point. The integrity is the pitch. *(Source: the 100% model and its critique, charity:water / CharityWatch.)*

**The container is the solicitation room.** Across major-gift practice the most reliable accelerant is the immersive experience that lets the donor feel the work before anyone asks. So never ask for money before the walk-through. The sequence is fixed: walk through, then a twenty-minute conversation about the next city, then `/contained/invest`. The one-sentence ask, in the open air just outside Room 3: *"Adelaide is stop two. The next city needs a backer. A stop is $30K. Would you put your name to one?"* The brief is the follow-up, never the front door. *(Source: immersion-then-ask, the cultivation cycle, Blackbaud / AFP.)*

**The proof loop, made physical.** Build a one-page stewardship loop for every funded stop: what the $30K bought (the build, the young people paid to host, the walk-throughs delivered, the decision-makers who came), who walked through, what they said, where the container goes next. This is charity:water's report card applied to a container, and Australia already has the systems-change version of it that these funders believe in: Maranguka in Bourke, Aboriginal-led, seed-funded by Dusseldorp and VFFF, returned roughly five times its operating cost, $3.1M in a single year (KPMG). Lead the funder/government segment with the Australian precedent, the money moves rather than vanishes, and link `justicehub.com.au/contained/brief` rather than quoting numbers you would later have to defend. *(Source: proof loop and "you did this", charity:water; the reinvestment-maths precedent, Maranguka / KPMG.)*

**The belonging engine: a backers' circle, fed by the tour.** charity:water's monthly community, The Spring, churns far less than a campaign because donors belong to something. Build a small "backers of the tour" circle, the 7 Partner/committed plus whoever funds Adelaide, fed the daily Adelaide recap as insiders and the after-each-stop proof loop. The cadence is the relationship: every interaction recorded, every next move pre-scheduled, no funder ever blasted. Six funder-lane contacts already received the March retired-figure blast and none replied, a bulk send is the opposite of a belonging engine. *(Source: The Spring monthly community, charity:water.)*

**The peer multiplier: backers recruit backers.** Point the nominate mechanic at funders: "I funded Adelaide. Who funds Perth?" A single founder cannot out-fundraise a network of advocates. Hand any live warm multiplier the specific thing to carry, the Perth stop at $30K and the local anchor, rather than a vague "help us spread the word." Consider a pitch at a live peer-funding venue (The Funding Network's TFN Live) for a room of pre-warmed donors. The mechanic must always route back to a walk-through and a $30K ask, never just an applause meter. *(Source: give-it-forward / birthday mechanic, charity:water; live peer pitch, The Funding Network.)*

**Cadence per named relationship.** Match each funder to its rung and never skip a stage. Stewardship gets the proof loop and the next-city ask. Cultivation gets a warm-intro walk-through invite. Identification gets the human found behind the front-desk inbox before any ask. A named warm contact is a recoverable lead, the move is a personal walk-through invite, then qualify capacity in the twenty-minute conversation. Never fabricate a commitment or an institutional detail about any real person.

## 5. National scale arc: Adelaide as proof, the tour as a movement

Adelaide on Tuesday 23 June is not a launch you repeat. It is stop two of a long walk that already started at Mount Druitt and continues. Michael Long's Long Walk worked because anyone could join at any depth, walk a kilometre or walk the whole way, and the low-threshold entry opened onto a high-commitment identity. CONTAINED's container is its Long Walk, the escalation ladder is "walk for an hour or walk the whole way," and each city that hosts is joining the walk, not buying a franchise.

**The container is the gold paint.** Shared Studios' Portals connected sixty thousand people across twenty countries because the gold-painted shipping container in Kigali and the one in New York were legibly the same thing, one initiative, not scattered local art. The same object, the same three rooms, the same thirty minutes, recognisable from Mount Druitt to Kaurna Yarta (Adelaide) to Boorloo (Perth), Mparntwe (Alice Springs), Meanjin (Brisbane), Warrane (Sydney), Naarm (Melbourne), nipaluna (Hobart) and the Northern Rivers, is what makes a national tour feel like one movement rather than ten unrelated events. What stays constant is the object and the three-room spine. What changes is Room 3, the local wall. *(Source: one recognisable travelling object, Portals.)*

**JusticeHub is the clearing house.** The Campaign for Youth Justice did not run every state fight, it armed each state coalition with shared evidence and framing and let each win its own. JusticeHub holds the evidence and the standard, each tour city wins its own version, point hosts to `justicehub.com.au/contained/brief` rather than re-arguing the numbers city by city. *(Source: arm each coalition, hold the centre, Campaign for Youth Justice.)*

**Each city inherits a hub-kit, never a parachute.** The Missouri Model's honest finding is that a perfect proof is widely cited and seldom replicated, because replication needs a committed local champion, not admiration. So never tour to a city without a named host who can hold it: if we cannot host it, we do not open it. What each city inherits, bottled into the next-city trigger pack: the $30K-a-stop maths, the three-room method, the consent protocol, the §7 figures, the voice rules, the org-wall template, and the soil-jar token (one identical token travels to the next city's Room 3, so Adelaide's vessel and Perth's vessel are visibly the same movement). What each city authors locally: its own Room 3 wall, governed by its own community orgs the way Bourke's plan is governed by the Tribal Council. The tour deposits, it does not extract, every stop must leave a named local relationship and a path for that city's orgs to stay on the wall, or the tour is a tour of spikes. *(Source: "seldom replicated" replication warning, Missouri Model / Casey; federated soil-jar structure, EJI; deposit-not-extract, The Long Walk.)*

**Announce the tour as a dated season.** Movember sustains where Ice Bucket spiked because you can predict it, a fixed calendar slot people anticipate and re-enter. Announce tour stops as a season cities can prepare for, so each city's organisers can build their Room 3 wall in advance and funders can anticipate the next one. *(Source: recurring ritual / calendar, Movember.)*

## 6. Failure modes to design against, and the guardrails

The graveyard of immersive advocacy is full of experiences that moved everyone and changed nothing. Each failure below is named with its guardrail.

1. **The Kony cliff: virality with no capture layer.** Nineteen thousand Sydney registrations, twenty-five attendees, because the feeling landed on a form with nothing behind it. CONTAINED's live version of this is the empty Adelaide booking funnel ten days out and the fourteen inbound contacts never told the container is happening. **Guardrail:** fix the catcher before opening the tap. Fill the funnel by hand this week off `roster.json`, reply personally to the fourteen, capture every walk-through at the threshold. Measure rung-movement, never impressions. Never widen to the polluted ~272 tag query chasing scale.

2. **Defer the ask and lose it.** Conversion happens in seconds at the threshold, not in an email days later. **Guardrail:** a trained young host at the exit with a physical nomination card and the locked door-opener question. The post-experience cron is the backup, never the front line, especially while `EMAIL_ENABLED` is unverified.

3. **A menu of asks at peak emotion.** Five options produce paralysis. **Guardrail:** one named rung per visitor, pre-assigned in the CRM by relationship owner, decided before the door opens.

4. **Savior framing: making affected people the subject, not the author.** Kony made Ugandans the backdrop to American action and imploded in two weeks. **Guardrail:** Indigenous-led, community-controlled orgs as authors and consent-givers on the Room 3 wall, young people paid and consenting as hosts, "for them, not about them." Route every community-controlled org through a named relationship, never a cold inbox blast. Never start and end inside detention, always arrive at the alternative.

5. **Oversimplification that shatters on contact with an expert.** "The cure already exists" is a strong simple line, and a simple line must sit on verifiable ground. **Guardrail:** JusticeHub carries the evidence claims, the container carries only the feeling. The §7 figure discipline (source, year, jurisdiction, retired numbers banned) is the anti-Kony immune system. Hold the Diagrama wording gate, no percentage public without partner sign-off, a wrong stat from a partner is a credibility crack.

6. **Identity tourism and spectacle over witnessing.** A short immersion can turn a marginalised person into a privileged visitor's emotional souvenir, and the instant a young person's pain becomes a clip or a fundraising image, CONTAINED crosses from Sorry Day into extraction. **Guardrail:** nobody filmed inside, co-authors never case studies, run the "nothing about us without us" check before any surface ships. If a piece of content would make a young person an object, it does not ship.

7. **The one-time spike that never returns (Ice Bucket's weakness; the consolation plateau of It Gets Better).** A spike is not a base, and a story archive that only consoles, without converting to a demand, plateaus. **Guardrail:** the escalation ladder is the anti-one-timer instrument, every witness gets one named next rung within 48 hours, and every room ends on a name written down, never on "wasn't that powerful."

8. **The win that rides the government of the day (Queensland's reversal).** A statute the next election can unwind is fragile. **Guardrail:** build for norm change under the law. Measure CONTAINED in how many people changed how they talk at their own dinner tables and boardrooms, not only in a bookable-MP count. A person who hosts is harder to un-convince than a person who merely attended.

9. **The roadshow that arrives, performs, and leaves.** **Guardrail:** the tour deposits, it does not extract. Leave each city's wall, relationships and young people's work intact and credited, with a path for local orgs to stay on the wall.

10. **Tokenism: Aboriginal art on a settler document while the settler framework runs underneath.** **Guardrail:** the wall must reflect real authority, real consent, and real pay where young people work. Decoration without control is the exact thing First Nations campaigners name and reject.

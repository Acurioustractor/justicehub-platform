## Executive summary

CONTAINED is ten days from doors and the gap is not the story, the brand, or the platform. Those are real and sound. The gap is the same one that decides every immersive campaign: the part that turns a feeling into a record, a reply, and a booking.

Here is where CONTAINED stands. The Engagement pipeline holds **78 warm, named people**, each with a relationship owner. They split four ways: **23 Identified** (the funder and target lane, outreach pending, zero personal CONTAINED touch), **34 Personal outreach** (the South Australian service-org wall, the orgs whose consent Room 3 depends on), **14 In conversation** (inbound hot leads who reached out and have not yet been told the container is happening in their country in ten days), and **7 Partner/committed** (the Diagrama team, Hannah March at the Justice Reform Initiative, Nicole Mekler at Just Reinvest, and others). Against that warm base sits the launch-deciding number: the **Adelaide booking funnel is empty**. Only test records. Ten days out, nobody is booked.

That empty funnel is the whole risk, and history names it exactly. Kony 2012 had nineteen thousand Sydney registrations and twenty-five people turned up, because the feeling was real and the machine to catch it did not exist. CONTAINED's defence is the inverse of Kony's weakness: a small, deliberate, high-intimacy experience with a real evidence layer underneath. But the defence only holds if the capture and the follow-up actually run this week, by hand, off the honest 78.

**The single thesis for winning.** Proximity is the product, and capture is the campaign. The container moves a person more than any report can. JusticeHub carries the proof. The escalation ladder gives every moved person one concrete next rung before the feeling fades: witness, then nominate a decision-maker, then stand with it, then host it in your city. So the ten days do exactly two things, in order. First, fill the room: turn the 78 warm relationships into booked walk-throughs and seed the empty Adelaide funnel by hand. Second, build the catcher: a working receipt, a durable capture row, a nominee path that does not silently fail, so that when a person stands inside and walks out changed, there is a hand at the threshold and a record that holds. Reach is the top of the ladder, never the prize. The win is rung-movement across 78 real people, not impressions across a vanity number.

## The PLAYBOOK: the engine and 90-day shape at a glance

Full strategy is **`02-campaign-playbook-best-in-history.md`**, drawn from the three research files. This is the engine.

CONTAINED is not selling tickets. It is a movement with a physical front door: one container, three rooms, thirty minutes, alone, phone and shoes left at the door, nobody filmed inside. The greatest justice and immersive campaigns of the last fifteen years all proved two things, in order, and CONTAINED's escalation ladder already names both: a room can move a person more than a report (witness), and a moved person does nothing unless the room hands them the next concrete thing to do before the feeling fades (nominate, stand, host).

### The six plays that win, and where each comes from

1. **Proximity is the product.** Bryan Stevenson's Legacy Museum does not argue you into a position, it stands you on the site and lets the floor do the work. The container is that move at a fraction of the cost. Protect the thirty-minute solo walkthrough fiercely. Never let the launch turn it into a backdrop for a panel. Every ask to the 78 is "who needs to stand inside this before their next decision?", never "promote our event."

2. **Feel, then act, never end inside the cell.** Van Jones's #cut50 carried humanisation and legislation at once, in that order: feel, then act. The container humanises, JusticeHub legislates. Always arrive at Room 3 and the action. The walkthrough without a rung is Kony 2012; the rung without the walkthrough is a committee. Carry both, in that order.

3. **The org is the messenger, the young person is never extracted.** The Campaign for Youth Justice put the directly-impacted parent at the front, not the think tank. Maranguka in Bourke held authority through the Bourke Tribal Council, not participation. For CONTAINED the lives are Aboriginal children, so the rule is absolute: no name, image, or story without confirmed consent on their terms, young people only ever through their organisations. The 34 SA orgs are the trusted witnesses; Room 3 is their co-authored wall.

4. **Carry the existence proof plus the reinvestment maths.** Missouri closed its youth prisons and rebuilt around relationships, the existence proof you cannot argue out of a building. Maranguka saved government over $3 million and cut under-25 unlicensed-driving arrests 72%. Room 2 is CONTAINED's Missouri, built on Diagrama's Spanish model. Lead the funder and government segment with the proof that the better thing already runs, and the maths that the money moves rather than vanishes ($3,261 a day to detain one SA child, Productivity Commission ROGS 2024-25). Prefer linking `justicehub.com.au/contained/brief` over quoting numbers. Diagrama percentages stay behind the partner wording gate until sign-off.

5. **Convert at the threshold, in human hands.** UNICEF's "Clouds Over Sidra" roughly doubled the face-to-face donation rate, from one in twelve to one in six, because the ask happened with the headset still warm and a human present. Stanford's "Becoming Homeless" got 85% petition signatures because the petition was right there. The conversion window is seconds, not days. CONTAINED's open envelope is a trained young host at the exit with a physical nomination card and a ten-second QR. The post-experience email is the backup, never the front line.

6. **One recognisable object travels, one heart, many cities, a named host in each.** Shared Studios' gold-painted Portals made a container in Kigali and a container in New York legibly the same movement. The container is CONTAINED's gold paint: same object, same three rooms, same thirty minutes, from Mount Druitt to Kaurna Yarta to Boorloo. JusticeHub is the clearing house that holds the evidence and the standard, the way the Campaign for Youth Justice armed each state coalition. Only Room 3's local wall changes. Never open a city without a host who can hold it. Missouri's hard lesson is that a perfect proof does not replicate without a local champion.

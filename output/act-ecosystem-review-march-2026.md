### Structural Gaps

1. **No revenue model yet.** The platform thesis says "governments and corporates pay for intelligence" but there's no pricing, no sales pipeline, no first customer beyond funder pitches.

2. **The Goods Project is isolated.** It's the most visceral proof point in the ecosystem but it's not data-connected to anything. A Goods delivery to a community served by an ALMA Basecamp org would be the most powerful cross-platform story possible.

3. **Oonchiumpa is one Basecamp.** The model needs 3-5 operating to prove it's not personality-dependent. QLD (Palm Island) and NSW (Mountie Yarns) are confirmed but not activated.

4. **The 98K orgs are mostly noise.** 74,892 are "community adjacent" (small businesses from QGIP). The meaningful universe is probably ~5,000 orgs. The scale is impressive for a slide but the signal is in the ~2,000 Indigenous orgs and ~1,000 programs.

## What the Shape Suggests

I'm not going to tell you what to do. But here's what the data suggests is possible:

### The Immediate Compound Effect (weeks)

When a Goods delivery happens to a community, pull up that community's JusticeHub profile: orgs operating there, funding they receive, programs running, governance network. That's a story Teya, Lucy, and every funder in the country would share.

### The Medium-Term Position (months)

The Community Evidence Passport from the Power Shift Compact. A community org proves its capability once — programs, outcomes, cost data, governance, stories — and that proof travels with them to every tender, every funder meeting, every policy submission. JusticeHub generates it. GrantScope validates the funding history. CivicScope tracks the government promises it responds to. Empathy Ledger provides the human evidence. The Basecamp page IS the passport.

No other org in Australia can issue that passport because no other org holds all five layers.

### The Long-Term Thesis (years)

You're building the infrastructure for a fundamental reallocation. Not asking government to do better — making it impossible for them to pretend they don't know. When every dollar is traced, every promise tracked, every outcome measured, every alternative proven, and every human story told — the political cost of inaction exceeds the political cost of change.

That's what $120B in traced funding, 1,845 unimplemented recommendations, and a kid who needs a bed adds up to.

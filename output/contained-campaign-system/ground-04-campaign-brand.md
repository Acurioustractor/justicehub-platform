## 1. Core story & messaging hierarchy

**The one line:** *One container. Three rooms. The cure already exists.*

The reality of youth detention, the evidence for change, and the community-led alternatives already doing the work.

**Secondary line (the platform handoff):** *CONTAINED shows what youth detention feels like. JusticeHub shows what works instead.*

**Legacy print line (still valid):** *One shipping container. Three rooms. Thirty minutes.*

**Expanded line:** *CONTAINED moves people emotionally. JusticeHub turns that attention into public evidence, community visibility, funding pathways, and political pressure.*

The hierarchy: emotion first (the container), evidence second (JusticeHub), action third (back what works). Never start and end inside detention — always arrive at the alternative.

## 3. The three rooms

| Room | Title | Duration | What it is | Tone |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Room 1** | Current Reality | 10 min | Sensory recreation of what a 15-year-old experiences inside an Australian youth detention centre. Concrete, strip lighting, isolation. Designed by young people at each stop. | critical |
| **Room 2** | What Works | 10 min | Based on the visit to Diagrama Foundation's youth justice centres in Spain. Education, therapy, connection instead of concrete. | transitional |
| **Room 3** | The Organisations Already Doing It | 10 min | Celebrates the grassroots Indigenous-led and community orgs doing the work. **Changes each stop** to feature the local host org. In Adelaide: a wall of SA organisations. | hopeful |

Thirty minutes total, alone, phone and shoes at the door, nobody filmed, nothing asked of you inside.

## 5. Audience segments — what each cares about, the ask, the channel

Story is constant. The ask and the "need to know" change. (Source: audience map.)

1. **Courts / MPs / policy (Priority A walkthroughs).** Care: 30 min, private, safe, evidence they can cite, nobody films them. Ask: book one slot before their next decision about a young person. Channel: personal outreach only, never bulk. Door-opener: *"Who needs to walk through this before they make the next decision?"*
2. **Funders & philanthropy.** Care: Mount Druitt already happened, Adelaide is stop two, $30K/stop, $50–70K/container — the machine exists, backing extends it. Ask: walk through, then a 20-min conversation about the next city (`/contained/invest`). Channel: personal invite first; `funder-brief` stream (~40) for the post-week brief.
3. **Media.** Care: the visual is a container in public space built by young people; the story is the cure already exists. Interview windows Wed + Thu am. Consent rules strict and handled. Ask: cover it, walk it, interview the builders. Channel: `media-pack` stream (~32) + personal pitch to priority journalists; media page `/contained/tour/social`.
4. **Youth justice services & practitioners.** Care: this is *for* them, not *about* them; Room 3 sends visitors toward services. Ask: walk through, bring a budget/caseload colleague, get their org on the SA wall (with approval). Channel: `youth-justice-brief` stream (~63) + JRI / conference networks.
5. **Conference delegates (Reintegration Puzzle, 24–25 June).** Care: short walk from the conference, bookable around sessions, carries a question back into the room. Ask: book a break slot; take the question in. Channel: targeted booking link. **BLOCKED:** 0 delegate contacts until JRI/Puzzle provides the import; until then JRI carries the invitation inside.
6. **Young people & youth orgs.** Care: young people lead this; paid greeter/host roles with support, supervision, breaks; nothing enters without consent on their terms. Ask: nominate young people for paid host shifts; partner on consent pathways. Channel: direct to org leaders (SYC, Unfiltered, Mounty Yarns network, Kate Bjur's Brisbane group), never bulk, never cold to young people. This is **workstream 3**.
7. **Warm public (the 28, + walk-ups).** Care: free, 30 min, exact booked time, phone + shoes at the door, limited windows. Ask: book, and bring one person who would never normally come. Channel: `newsletter-stream:contained-adelaide-invite` (~28, eligibility-gated) + QR at the container + waitlist.

**The escalation ladder (Beautiful Trouble):** Witness → Nominate → Back → Share → Host → Fund → Document. Every surface needs a distinct rung.

**Three action pillars:** Fund a Stop ($30K) · Spread the Word (share evidence, contact your MP) · Host the Container (venue / festival / community space).

## 6. Brand colours (LOCKED) & typography

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Black | `#0A0A0A` | Body text, headings, primary backgrounds (warmth, not `#000000`) |
| Off-White | `#F5F0E8` | Page backgrounds, card surfaces — the default, NOT `#FFFFFF` |
| Urgent Red | `#DC2626` | CTAs + critical data ONLY. Never decorative. One intense moment beats five. |
| Emerald | `#059669` | Success metrics, positive outcomes, community-led indicators |

Rules: one colour accent per section · red reserved for CTAs/critical stats · never pure white or pure black · never pure gray for body text.

**Typography:**
- **Display — Space Grotesk Bold (700)**, tight tracking (`-0.05em`). Hero 48–72px, section 32–40px, sub 24–28px.
- **Data & labels — IBM Plex Mono Regular (400) / Medium (500).** Data callouts 14–18px, captions/meta 12–14px.
- Maximise contrast between heavy display and light label weight. Asymmetric scale: large headline next to small muted data.

**Visual style:** Bauhaus / constructivist — high contrast, angular, clip-paths and diagonal cuts, brutalist grid with generous whitespace, red wedge accent used sparingly. Information on surfaces, not boxed in cards.

CONTAINED runs its OWN locked sub-brand (Space Grotesk / IBM Plex Mono / the four hexes above). For CONTAINED surfaces, the brand-guide palette wins. Do not mix the two systems on a CONTAINED page.

## 8. Voice — Imagination Architect (default for narrative copy)

Seed: *"Craft visionary narratives through poetic and systemic communication that inspires transformative thinking."*

**Use for:** pitch pages, briefs, articles, funder emails, opinion pieces, public campaign copy.
**Don't use for:** technical docs, schema, code comments, admin pages, error messages.

**Four pillars:**
1. **Visionary** — write from a future that already has roots. Reframe before describing ("Not a grant. A named partnership"). Tense interplay: present + future + already-happened, woven.
2. **Poetic** — short / longer / short / longest sentence rhythm; end on a beat. Sensory anchor every abstraction (the kettle, on Country at Atnarpa, phone and shoes at the door). Names over abstractions (Kristy, Tanya — not "the leadership team"). One quotable sentence per major section.
3. **Systemic** — name the architecture before the parts (rings, anchors, layers, vehicles, surfaces, holds, carries). Money lives inside structure. *Money flows inward. Evidence flows outward. Story stays sovereign at the centre.*
4. **Transformative** — open each section by reframing the question, not answering it. Put the reader's objection inside the answer. One frame-shift per section.

**Words to use:** hold, carry, see, anchor, ring, weave, surface, ground, root, gather · already, in motion, underway, on Country, in practice · partnership (not grant), partner (not funder), name (not credit).

### AI-tell blocklist (zero tolerance)
- **Em-dashes:** zero `—` characters. Use commas, full stops, colons, or mid-dot `·`.
- **Vocab to delete on sight:** delve, crucial, pivotal, seamless, robust, comprehensive, nuanced, multifaceted, holistic, tapestry, landscape, navigate, ecosystem (as metaphor), stakeholder, leverage, synergy, strategic, scalable, scale up, "it is important to note that".
- **Sentence frame to kill:** "Not just X but Y."
- **No rule-of-three padding**, no negative parallelism.
- **JusticeHub never-words:** "Minderoo's research" (always: Front Project, supported by Minderoo) · "Indigenous communities" in the abstract (always name the country / language group / organisation) · "best practice" · "beneficiaries" (community orgs are heroes; young people are co-authors).
- Curly quotes in body copy; straight quotes only in code.

### Email & outreach voice (Ben, LOCKED)
Human, warm, vulnerable, real. First-name casual greeting ("Hey", "Quick one"). Honest about what isn't figured out yet. Momentum shown with specifics (real places, numbers, names). Soft ask ("keen to grab a coffee"). End human ("Hope to talk soon"). **Never:** "I hope this email finds you well", "Kind regards", "I'm reaching out to", "I wanted to touch base".

### Language guardrails (always / never)
- **Always:** "system-impacted young people"; "community-led alternatives"; lead with what works; centre community authority; Indigenous leadership explicit; evidence is settled, the question is political will.
- **Never:** "offenders" / "juvenile delinquents"; "at-risk" without naming who defined the risk; "tough on crime" framing (even to critique); sensationalised language about young people; front-loading stark stats without narrative context; imply JusticeHub ranks or profiles young people.

### Seven tests (run before shipping any narrative draft)
Reframe · Name · Image · Rhythm · Architecture · Em-dash (search `—`) · AI-vocab (search the blocklist).

## 10. CTA framework

This is NOT ticket-selling. CONTAINED is a movement that needs backing.

| Instead of | Use |
|---|---|
| Get Tickets / Book Now | **Back This** / **Back This Stop** |
| Register | **Support This Stop** |
| Now Showing | **Funded** |
| Coming Soon | **Seeking Support** |
| See It For Yourself | **Help Us Get There** |

Three CTA buckets (every page ties to one): **Experience it** (book your 30 min) · **Nominate a decision-maker** (public pressure) · **Back / Host the tour** (fund the movement). The ask is never abstract — the ask is to back what works.

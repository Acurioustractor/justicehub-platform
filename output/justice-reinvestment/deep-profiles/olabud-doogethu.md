# Olabud Doogethu Aboriginal Corporation (Halls Creek, WA)

Deep organisational profile. Compiled 2026-06-11 from web sources. Olabud Doogethu is a Kriol phrase translating as "all of us together". It is Western Australia's first justice reinvestment site, based in Halls Creek in the East Kimberley.

**Important registration finding:** the ABN supplied for this profile (47 629 577 245) is correct for the entity, but ABN Lookup historical records show the corporation was registered as "Olabud Doogethu Aboriginal Corporation" from 3 March 2021 to 10 February 2026, and was renamed **"Marlagowyah Aboriginal Corporation"** on 10 February 2026. The Olabud Doogethu name continues as the program and public brand. The rename is verified against ABR only; no media coverage or organisational announcement explaining it was found. ## 1. History

- **Origins under the Shire of Halls Creek.** Olabud Doogethu was created by the Shire of Halls Creek together with the Aboriginal communities of the Shire, with support from Social Reinvestment WA, a state-wide coalition of NGOs. It is consistently described as Western Australia's first justice reinvestment site, co-designed and co-led by 11 Aboriginal communities and the Shire. The 11 communities are Halls Creek township plus Wirrimanu (Balgo), Mindibungu (Billiluna), Paruku (Mulan), Kundat Djaru (Ringer Soak), Warmun (Turkey Creek), Yiyili, Nicholson Block, Lundja (Red Hill), Yardgee and Mardiwah Loop. Sources: Olabud Doogethu website, Shire of Halls Creek, ACT Community Services attachment, Justice Reinvestment Network Australia (JRNA).
- **Consultative process (circa 2018).** The project began with a consultative process that gave Elders, representatives and community members from each remote community in the Shire a pathway to gather and decide priorities for change and action. The project frames itself as a long term "process" rather than a "project", with Indigenous sovereignty and empowerment at the centre. Source: olabuddoogethu.org.au (About Us, The Framework). The exact start date of consultations was not pinned to a month in available sources. GAP: needs manual research (the Shire's October 2019 publication, listed in Sources, likely contains the precise timeline but returned HTTP 403).
- **2019: launch and first programs.** The Youth Engagement Night Officer (YENO) program, the flagship, began in 2019 with Aboriginal Elders volunteering their time to walk the streets at night. Media reporting (AAP via Canberra Times, December 2022) also dates the program launch to 2019. Sources: olabuddoogethu.org.au, Canberra Times.
- **3 March 2021: incorporation.** The entity's ABN (47 629 577 245) became active on 3 March 2021 under the name Olabud Doogethu Aboriginal Corporation, marking the transition from a Shire-auspiced project to its own Aboriginal corporation registered under the CATSI Act. Source: ABN Lookup. The corporation is an "Other Incorporated Entity", main business location postcode 6770 (Halls Creek), not registered for GST, and not DGR endorsed.
- **May 2021: WA Police outcome data.** WA Police provided the corporation with data showing large reductions in youth offending indicators (detail in section 2). By around this time the organisation reported nearly 40 local Aboriginal staff. Sources: corporation presentation hosted at indigenousjustice.gov.au; JRNA.
- **June 2023: federal justice reinvestment funding.** The Commonwealth (Attorney-General Mark Dreyfus and Minister for Indigenous Australians Linda Burney) announced justice reinvestment funding for Halls Creek, supporting Olabud Doogethu's work with young Aboriginal men, fathers and sons. The dollar amount was not stated in retrieved material. GAP: needs manual research (amount and duration of the Commonwealth grant). Source: ministers.ag.gov.au media release, 15 June 2023.
- **10 February 2026: renamed Marlagowyah Aboriginal Corporation** (see note above). Source: ABN Lookup historical record.
- **Key people named across sources:** Dean Mosquito (Kija and Juru man, Executive Officer for Culture and Transformation; also reported as a director in 2022), Arron Little (director, 2022), Kevin Hunter (supervisor of the Youth Engagement Night Officers, 2022), Keith Jugarie (Kija and Jaru Elder, established the Men's Tribal Centre), and YENO officers Joseph Cox (Jaru and Gooniyandi man), Doug Morgan (Balanggarra man) and Thomas Bradshaw ("Junior T"). Sources: JRNA, Canberra Times, olabuddoogethu.org.au. UNVERIFIED: whether these roles are current in 2026.

## 2. Evidence and reviews

The widely cited "63%" figure is real but is often misquoted. The primary citation is the corporation's own presentation "Smart Justice in the Heart of the Kimberley" (Olabud Doogethu Aboriginal Corporation, published on the Australian Institute of Criminology's Indigenous Justice Clearinghouse site, 2021), which attributes all figures to **WA Police data provided in May 2021**:

| Indicator | Change | Age group | Period |
|---|---|---|---|
| Burglaries | -63% | 10 to 17 | 2017-20 |
| Arrests made | -69% | 10 to 14 | 2017-20 |
| Referrals to Juvenile Justice Team | -63% | 10 to 17 | 2018-20 |
| Aboriginal persons admitted to police custody | -64% | 10 and over | 2018-20 |
| Motor vehicle thefts | -59% | 10 to 17 | 2017-20 |

Notes on this evidence:

- **What the 63% is:** a 63 per cent reduction in burglaries committed by 10 to 17 year olds in Halls Creek between 2017 and 2020, per WA Police data supplied to the corporation in May 2021. It is police administrative data describing change over time, not a controlled evaluation, and the corporation presents it as such ("through Olabud Doogethu's efforts we have seen massive improvements"). Verified directly against the presentation PDF (pages read on 2026-06-11).
- **Secondary citations:** the ACT Community Services directorate reproduced the figures in a published attachment (it quotes 58 per cent for motor vehicle theft where the corporation's own slide says 59 per cent, and describes the arrests figure as ages 10 to 17 where the slide says 10 to 14; treat the corporation's slide as authoritative). JRNA also reproduces the May 2021 WA Police figures and adds a 43 per cent reduction in oral cautions (aged 10 to 14) in 2019-20.
- **A different early figure also circulates:** "burglaries reduced by 58% in the first 18 months", cited by JRNA and Social Reinvestment WA material. This is an earlier snapshot (roughly 2019 to mid 2020) and a different measurement window from the 63 per cent 2017-20 figure. The two are frequently conflated.
- **Counter-evidence on durability:** AAP reporting (December 2022) recorded that offences against property in Halls Creek rose from 595 in 2020-21 to 863 in 2021-22, with program staff acknowledging crime fell after the 2019 launch but rose again. Any use of the 63 per cent figure should note it covers 2017-20 only.
- **No independent published evaluation was located.** The October 2019 Shire publication ("The Olabud Doogethu Project", Shire of Halls Creek) is the main early program document. No formal external evaluation (university or government commissioned, with named authors) was found in this pass. GAP: needs manual research (check whether the federal National Justice Reinvestment Program's evaluation arrangements or Social Reinvestment WA have since published an evaluation).

## 3. Impact model

- **Youth Engagement Night Officers (YENO).** The flagship. Local Aboriginal community leaders and Elders patrol the streets at night (reported five nights a week in 2022), engaging children who are out at night, some as young as six, keeping them safe and connected rather than entering the justice system. It began as volunteer Elder patrols in 2019 and became paid local employment. Named officers include Joseph Cox, Doug Morgan and Thomas Bradshaw.
- **Program architecture (per the corporation's presentation and ACT government summary).** Two streams. "Justice Circuit Breaker" (youth justice): Youth Engagement Night Officers, Family Support (Case Intervention), and Mibala (alternative education, a culturally led learning on Country pathway designed by Elders to re-engage at-risk young people into schooling or jobs). "Human Rights Department" (Nothing About Us Without Us): Kutjungka (Community Navigators), the Men's Tribal Centre (Elders' reference group, being developed at the former Halls Creek golf course, a traditional ceremonial area), and Community Justice. The ACT summary also lists Make a Change (family and domestic violence support).
- **How they describe change.** Justice reinvestment as practised here means diverting public funds from incarceration into community-designed early intervention. The framework names six outcomes: localising solutions to root causes of offending, strengthening community safety and belonging, supporting families and young people on underlying issues, improving justice approaches through diversion, building culturally relevant community capacity, and collaborative local partnerships. The organisation's core claim is that "Aboriginal led governance, Aboriginal led decision making, and Aboriginal led program design works". Local Aboriginal employment is itself part of the model (nearly 40 local Aboriginal staff reported in 2021).
- **Redesigning justice.** With research partners the organisation describes work to recreate the local justice system based in Aboriginal law and culture and community-led strategy (the "Redesigning Justice in Halls Creek" workstream).

## 4. Current status and what's next

- **Legal entity:** renamed Marlagowyah Aboriginal Corporation on 10 February 2026 (ABN 47 629 577 245 remains active). The Olabud Doogethu website (olabuddoogethu.org.au) remains live and continues to present the program under the Olabud Doogethu name. UNVERIFIED: governance and leadership as of 2026; the website homepage lists no leadership names.
- **Funding:** mixed state and federal government funding plus philanthropic contributions, with the Shire of Halls Creek providing infrastructure, vehicles and housing support (as at the December 2022 reporting). Commonwealth justice reinvestment funding announced June 2023 for work with young Aboriginal men, fathers and sons. GAP: needs manual research (current funding agreements, and whether Halls Creek is a site under the Commonwealth's National Justice Reinvestment Program 2024-26 rounds).
- **Programs:** the corporation continues to describe early intervention, post-trauma support, education, training and career pathway programs, and community-driven rehabilitation. The stated trajectory in 2022 reporting was toward full independence from the Shire while maintaining service standards.
- **Pressures:** the 2021-22 rise in property offences in Halls Creek shows the operating environment remains volatile; staff in 2022 described more serious behaviour (rock throwing, car thefts) than at launch.

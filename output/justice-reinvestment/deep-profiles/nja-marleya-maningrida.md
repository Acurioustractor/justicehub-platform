## 1. History

### Deeper roots: Burnawarra and the Maningrida justice tradition

Maningrida's organised cultural justice work long predates the 2022 incorporation. The Burnawarra law and justice group, a body of prominent Elders and community leaders in Maningrida, was active for years before Nja-marleya existed. In 2017 the Burnawarra Justice Group made submissions to the Royal Commission into the Protection and Detention of Children in the Northern Territory, documenting the history of child removals in Maningrida and recommending local cultural education, early intervention and a strengthened kinship care system (Family Matters / SNAICC, 2017: https://www.familymatters.org.au/community-leaders-voice-their-concerns-about-child-protection-in-maningrida-arnhem-land/). The Burnawarra group was later "reinvigorated to work alongside police and the courts" under NT justice reforms (NT Attorney-General and Justice Department, Law and Justice Group guidelines: https://agd.nt.gov.au/attorney-general-and-justice/northern-territory-aboriginal-justice-agreement/guidelines-for-law-and-justice-groups).

Under the Northern Territory Aboriginal Justice Agreement, Maningrida became one of six officially recognised Law and Justice Groups in the NT (alongside Kintore, Groote Eylandt, Ramingining, Barunga/Wugularr and Yuendumu) (same NT AGD source). Law and Justice Group members are appointed by the CEO of the NT Department of Attorney-General and Justice and act as cultural advisers in sentencing.

The organisation's own account makes the continuity explicit: "Our Elders had a strong vision to return the cultural justice system to Maningrida, where Bininj and Balanda systems work together for positive impact" (Nja-marleya, NT Aboriginal Law and Justice Groups Forum 2026 listing: https://events.humanitix.com/communitylawandjusticeforum). GAP: the precise formal relationship between the Burnawarra group and the Nja-marleya company (succession, absorption, or parallel operation) is not documented in any source found; needs manual research, ideally by asking the organisation or NAAJA.

### The Gunawuna Jungai connection (tested, partly verified)

The hypothesis was that Nja-marleya (Maningrida) and Gunawuna Jungai Limited (Doomadgee, QLD) were stood up by the same Commonwealth justice reinvestment readiness program. What the registers actually show:

- **Verified:** Nja-marleya's ABN is active from 18 August 2022; Gunawuna Jungai Limited (ABN 59 661 831 170) is active from 19 August 2022, one day later. Both are Australian public companies limited by guarantee, both were ACNC-registered on their ABN start dates, and both later became Commonwealth justice reinvestment grantees. (ABR: https://abr.business.gov.au/ABN/View?abn=59661706430 and https://abr.business.gov.au/ABN/View?abn=59661831170.)
- **Nuance:** the ACNs (661 706 430 and 661 831 170) are about 125,000 apart, which is roughly consistent with one day of national ASIC registrations rather than a same-batch, same-agent lodgement. The matching "59" ABN prefix is a checksum artefact, not evidence of sequence.
- **Context:** the registrations predate the National Justice Reinvestment Program's first grants (February 2024) and even the October 2022 federal budget that funded the program, but follow the Albanese Government's 2022 election commitment of $79 million for community-led justice reinvestment. Both communities incorporating dedicated justice reinvestment vehicles in the same week of August 2022 is consistent with both preparing for the announced Commonwealth program. Gunawuna Jungai's ABR location is QLD 4064 (Milton, Brisbane), suggesting professional advisers handled its registration; Nja-marleya's is NT 0822 (Maningrida).
- **Verdict: UNVERIFIED.** No document was found naming a shared readiness or incorporation support program behind both entities. The one-day registration gap is established fact; the common-origin explanation remains a plausible inference only. GAP: needs manual research (AGD justice reinvestment readiness support records, or direct enquiry to either organisation).

## 3. Impact model

Nja-marleya's stated vision is the return of a cultural justice system to Maningrida in which Bininj (Aboriginal) and Balanda (non-Aboriginal) systems work together to lower the crime rate and reduce repeat offending (organisation website: https://www.njamarleya.org.au/; Facebook page: https://www.facebook.com/p/Nja-marl%C3%A9ya-Cultural-Leaders-and-Justice-Group-61557418263490/). The name itself is the model: "Nja-marleya means we are the body of the community. Looking after people" (website).

Four published service areas (website):

1. **Justice Work**, including Community Courts training. In the NT Community Court model, senior Aboriginal Law and Justice Group members sit with the judge at sentencing of Aboriginal defendants who plead guilty and have community connection, present an Aboriginal Experience Report covering cultural and personal circumstances, and help shape rehabilitation and cultural healing plans; the judge keeps full sentencing power (NT Local Court: https://localcourt.nt.gov.au/court-updates/community-court-northern-territory). Maningrida was an expansion site after Groote Eylandt, and Community Court is now listed as available in Groote Eylandt, Kintore, Maningrida and Ramingining (same source plus NT AGD Law and Justice Group guidelines).
2. **Youth Services**, including school holiday programs (website). Search summaries of the AGD justice reinvestment page state Nja-marleya operates the Maningrida Youth Centre and works with judges and lawyers to support young people; UNVERIFIED against the page text directly because the AGD page timed out.
3. **Funding Community Led Change** (a small-grants or enabling function for community initiatives, per the website service listing).
4. **Cultural Advisory Service** (website).

**Reported outcomes:** none quantified in public sources yet. The strongest documented activity outcomes are operational: Community Court sitting in Maningrida, coordination of the 2025 Maningrida Youth Summit (12 to 15 August 2025, bringing together young people from Maningrida and surrounding homelands for leadership, culture and community workshops; West Arnhem Regional Council: https://westarnhem.nt.gov.au/news/2025-maningrida-youth-summit-our-voice-our-story-our-future), and convening the territory-wide forum described below. GAP: recidivism or diversion figures for Maningrida Community Court need manual research through NT AGD.

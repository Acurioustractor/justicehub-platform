# Contained — the carrying vehicle, costed

A shipping container. Three rooms. A cell that strips you to nothing. A second room that asks what justice could be. A third room that hands the conversation back to community. Built first by Ben Knight and Nic Marchesi as an art piece in Mount Druitt. Now ready to travel.

This pack is for Lucy to test inside Minderoo and with adjacent funders. It maps three costed tiers, a programmatic spine that holds across all three, and a clear month-twelve artefact so the partnership has something to hold up at the end of Year 1.

---

## The frame Minderoo can walk in with

Contained is community priming, not campaigning. The distinction matters. Lucy named on the call that the public-facing campaign work pauses for twelve months while internal advocacy strategy resolves. Contained sits on the other side of that line. Contained warms the room. Contained takes a young person who has been through detention, pays them as a facilitator, and lets a Member of Parliament walk through with the same map. Contained builds the public sentiment a campaign would later land inside.

When Justice Reform Initiative is ready in twelve to eighteen months to take a national message into Canberra, the artefacts Contained has been gathering across Western Australia, New South Wales, and South Australia become the evidence the campaign carries.

## The programmatic spine

Every tour stop, regardless of tier, runs the same six-element rhythm. The cost shifts. The shape does not.

**One. Co-design week with the local partner.** Three to five Aboriginal community-controlled organisations, plus a youth arts or cultural institution, plus a university chair where one is interested. Set the room. Set the questions. Set the programmatic week.

**Two. Community build.** Young people with lived experience are paid to recreate the cell, paint the second room, build the third room with their organisation. The build is the program. The product is the byproduct. Trades skills, art skills, trauma-informed storytelling, and a paid pathway for at least four young people per stop.

**Three. Public open weeks.** Three to four weeks of free public access. Ticketed for ticket-pricing data on appetite, free for community and school groups. Members of Parliament invited on a dedicated day. Universities invited on a dedicated day. Funders and philanthropists invited on a dedicated evening.

**Four. Community priming events inside the container.** Yarning circles. Exhibitions. Local artist evenings. The container becomes the third place for the conversation about youth justice for the duration. Not a press release. A presence.

**Five. Documentation and storytelling capture.** The community storytelling work runs in parallel for every stop. Photo essays. Audio. Transcripts. Per-storyteller consent. The pack of artefacts that is published into JusticeHub case studies and the Living Map. Year 1's tour produces the material Year 2's campaign carries.

**Six. Close-out and handover.** A facilitated close where the container moves on. The lived-experience facilitators stay paid for a wind-down month. The local partner inherits the materials, the relationships, and the funder list of every person who walked through and left a postcode.

## Programmatic engagement layer · the why for organisations and universities

Lucy asked specifically about the programmatic way Contained can be used with organisations and universities for story and engagement. Three packages sit on top of the tour. Each one is a way an external partner buys into a stop and brings their audience with them.

### Package A · Anchor partnership · $40K to $60K per stop

For Aboriginal community-controlled organisations, youth services, and frontline organisations.

What the organisation gets: a paid co-design week. Up to four young people from their organisation paid as program facilitators for the six-week stop. Room 3 dedicated to their work, designed with them. A documented case study published into JusticeHub. Three film shorts about the organisation. A photo essay set. A bound copy of the year's national volume.

What Contained gets: cultural authority, local relationships, audience, and a credible Room 3 that does not parachute in.

This is the package the four anchors and the next tier of partner organisations sit inside. It is also the package the Western Australia delegated-authority pilot communities can buy into, paid for by Minderoo or by the Department of Justice in their portion of the program.

### Package B · University partnership · $25K to $40K per stop

For UWA, Flinders, Adelaide, UNSW, USyd, the Queensland universities, RMIT, Tasmania.

What the university gets: a dedicated university day inside the public open weeks. Up to two paid research positions for postgraduates to embed alongside the stop and produce one peer-review-bound study from the experience. Access to the documentation pack as licensed teaching material. A named seat on the year-end exhibition curation panel.

What Contained gets: a research credibility layer, an audience of students, an academic peer-review pathway for the case studies, and the university's reputation in the room when politicians arrive.

Lucy named UWA, Flinders, Adelaide, UNSW, and a Brisbane university interest. Package B is the way these universities are turned from interested observers into paying partners.

### Package C · Civic partnership · $20K to $35K per stop

For local councils, mayors, libraries, and government departments who want the conversation in their patch.

What the civic partner gets: a hosted week of council and constituency events inside the container. A dedicated MP morning. An exhibition that lives on after the container has moved, in their library or town hall. A named civic-partner credit on the year volume.

What Contained gets: location, security, public-space permissions, civic legitimacy, and an MP turn-up rate that goes from twenty per cent to seventy per cent when the local mayor signs the invitations.

Lucy mentioned Mayor Sophie Bretag at City of Onkaparinga who has openly supported online. Package C is the way her support becomes a paid partnership rather than a social-media nod.

## Programmatic week, scheduled

A typical six-week activation, end to end. The same shape across all tiers.

| Week | Inside the container | Outside the container |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Co-design week with anchor, university, civic partner | Crew arrival, fit-out, lived-experience facilitator induction |
| 2 | Build week, young people paid, Room 3 designed | Photo and audio capture for case studies begins |
| 3 | Public open week one, school groups, free public access | First MP day, first university day |
| 4 | Public open week two, community priming evenings, yarning circles | Filmmaker week, three short-film shoots |
| 5 | Public open week three, philanthropy evening, anchor case-study launch | Editorial week, transcripts and consent verification |
| 6 | Wind-down week, exhibition handover to local partner | Move logistics, post-stop reporting, next-stop set-up |

Lived-experience facilitators are paid through the wind-down. The local anchor inherits the artefacts, the audience list, and a budget line for one community priming event a month for the rest of the calendar year.

## Reversibility, named

Every tier ends at a clean review point. If the work is not working, Minderoo steps out without a cliff.

**Tier 1 review at month six.** Was the Perth activation worth replicating? If yes, Tier 2 starts. If no, the case-study volume is the partnership outcome and the container returns to Mount Druitt.

**Tier 2 review at month twelve.** Did the priority three rotation produce the artefacts and the public-sentiment shift the partnership was after? If yes, Tier 3 builds the second container and brings funder consortium partners in. If no, the three case-study volumes stand on their own and the container winds down.

**Tier 3 review at month twelve.** Has the national arc shifted the conversation Justice Reform Initiative can land into in Year 2? If yes, the year-end exhibition becomes a permanent travelling installation funded by a successor partnership. If no, the artefacts and the published case studies stand as the partnership outcome.

Lucy's organisation cannot make a three-year forward commitment until the Minderoo board approves. Every tier above is sized so a one-year commitment is itself a complete partnership outcome. Three years would be the unfolding. One year is the floor.

## What we are not asking Minderoo to do

We are not asking Minderoo to fund a research grant. The container does not need a logic model written before it builds.

We are not asking Minderoo to fund a campaign. Lucy was clear on the twelve-month pause. Contained is community priming, not campaign infrastructure.

We are not asking Minderoo to fund a launch event. The container is not a press release. It is a presence.

We are not asking Minderoo to be the only funder. Tier 1 and Tier 2 can be carried by Minderoo alone. Tier 3 is built for a consortium because that is the right shape for a national arc.

---

## What we are asking Minderoo to do

Carry one tier. Choose the shape. Tier 1 puts a Perth case study on the desk. Tier 2 puts the priority three on the road for twelve months. Tier 3 takes the national year on as a consortium with Minderoo holding the spine.

Whichever tier Lucy lands inside, the Reintegration Puzzle Conference at Tandanya in late June is the test event. We will be there. The question for Minderoo is whether Contained arrives at the conference under the Minderoo Communities pillar or whether it arrives self-funded. Either way, the container is there.

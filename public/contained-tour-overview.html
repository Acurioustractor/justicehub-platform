Where We're Going

A shipping container touring Australia — bringing youth justice data into communities, connecting local services with supporters, and collecting the stories the system doesn't tell.

At each stop, the container surfaces local youth justice data — which programs exist, where funding goes, what the evidence says — and collects stories from the people who live inside those numbers. It connects the organisations doing the work with the people who can support them — philanthropic, government, academic, and community.

Perth, WA

UWA + JRI Perth
Western Australia housed children in Unit 18 — an adult maximum-security prison. The Banksia Hill crisis saw kids moved into conditions designed for the state's most dangerous adult offenders. The human cost of this is not captured in any government dataset. WA has 9 mapped youth justice programs — including the Olabud Doogethu justice reinvestment site in Halls Creek.

What you'll see inside the container

Interactive data from JusticeHub and CivicScope — the intelligence tools powering each stop.

The 550:1 Ratio
Community programs cost a fraction of detention. Every verified program plotted on an interactive scatter chart — showing the gap between what works and where the money goes.
Funding Deserts
360 Local Government Areas classified by funding level. 96 are "funding deserts" — communities where detention spending dwarfs community investment.
Who Funds Whom
Billions in youth justice funding tracked across 35 sources. See exactly where the money goes — and where it doesn't.
Political Accountability
CivicScope tracks what politicians say about youth justice — ministerial statements, Hansard, consultancy spending. Announcements vs reality.
Network Explorer
587,000 entities and 1.5 million relationships. Search any organisation and see its funding connections, board members, and political donations.
Real Stories
From the Empathy Ledger — real stories from real people connected to the data points. The numbers have names.

How you can help

You don't need to be in the sector. You just need to give a damn.

Show up

Every share reaches someone we haven't met yet. This movement grew from people forwarding a post. Keep it going.

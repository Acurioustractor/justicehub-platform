03 Shapes & Visual Language
Bauhaus. Constructivist. Brutalist grid.
Angular compositions, high contrast, generous whitespace. The red wedge is our signature accent — used once per surface, never repeated.

04 Voice & Language
How we speak.
Direct. Evidence-backed. Urgent but never sensational. Every statistic has a source. The ask is never abstract — the ask is to back what works.

● Community authority first

The communities most affected deserve the biggest say. We centre their knowledge, not our analysis of it. Community organisations control their data.

● Evidence is story, not surveillance

Research is indexed without profiling individuals. Numbers earn their place by being sourced. Stories carry context that data alone cannot.

● Centre agency, not trauma

Lead with what works, not what's broken. Young people are experts in their own experience. Their authority drives the campaign.

● The ask is concrete

Not "reform the system." Instead: nominate a decision maker, book an experience, back the tour. Every call to action has a URL.

Always

  • System-impacted young people
  • Community-led alternatives
  • Justice infrastructure
  • Lead with what works
  • Every claim backed by a named source
  • This isn't a lecture, it's an experience
  • Real photos of real people

Never

  • "Offenders" or "juvenile delinquents"
  • "At-risk" without qualifying who defined risk
  • "Tough on crime" framing (even to critique)
  • AI-generated photorealistic images
  • Sensationalised language about young people
  • Unsourced statistics
  • Framing communities as broken or needing rescue

ALMA-Friendly Checklist

Every post, email, or page should pass these checks:
✓ Does every statistic have a named source?
✓ Are community voices centred before policy analysis?
✓ Does it lead with what works, not what's broken?
✓ Is the call to action concrete (URL, not platitude)?
✓ Are all photos real (not AI-generated photorealism)?
✓ Would the community being described approve this framing?
✓ Does it use "system-impacted" not "offenders"?

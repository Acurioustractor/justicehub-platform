# JusticeHub: Core Pitch for Mindaroo Foundation

**3-Year Investment:** $9.5M - $13.0M (BASE Scenario)
**Purpose:** Community-owned platform breaking philanthropic dependency
**Goal:** Indigenous sovereignty through economic self-determination

---

## The Problem

**Every young person** in Australia's youth justice system deserves better. But the crisis is most acute for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander young people—**17 times more likely** to be in detention than their non-Indigenous peers. The knowledge to change this exists in communities across Australia—Indigenous, CALD, remote, disability, and LGBTQIA+ communities—but it stays local, uncompensated, and invisible to the broader sector. Communities are trapped in grant dependency cycles while their proven solutions remain hidden.

**The result:** Fragmented knowledge, duplicated effort, perpetual philanthropic dependency across the entire youth justice sector.

---

## Our Solution: Community-Owned Intelligence Platform

JusticeHub creates **permanent digital infrastructure** serving **all young people** in Australia's youth justice system. Communities will legally own the platform by Year 5. This isn't another grant-funded program—it's **seed capital for economic self-determination**.

### Why Indigenous Leadership?

The platform is deliberately architected under **Indigenous leadership** for three reasons:

1. **Statistical urgency** — Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander young people are 17x overrepresented in detention, making this the sector's most acute crisis
2. **Knowledge sovereignty** — Indigenous communities have pioneered data sovereignty frameworks that set the gold standard for consent and community control
3. **Sector best practice** — If we build infrastructure that protects Indigenous knowledge properly, we protect everyone

**The platform serves all communities** working in youth justice—Indigenous, CALD, remote, disability, LGBTQIA+—with Indigenous governance ensuring the highest standards of consent, cultural safety, and community control for everyone.

### How It Works:

**Year 1:** Build platform, engage 6 communities (starting with Indigenous due to urgent need), train Indigenous developers
**Year 2:** Scale operations, reduce costs, grow community capacity
**Year 3:** Communities lead, platform generates revenue, ownership transfers

### What Makes This Different:

1. **True Community Ownership** — Platform legally transfers to community-governed ACCO by Year 5
2. **Breaks Dependency** — Revenue generation reduces philanthropic need from 100% → 30-40% by Year 5
3. **Fair Compensation** — All communities paid $180K annually (consultant rates, not token stipends)
4. **Indigenous Developers** — Young people trained and employed, replacing external contractors
5. **Data Sovereignty** — Indigenous sovereignty framework protects all communities
6. **Time-Limited** — 3-year partnership with clear exit strategy, not perpetual funding

### Who JusticeHub Serves

**Primary Beneficiaries:** All young people in Australia's youth justice system

**Communities We Engage:**
- **Indigenous communities** (Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander) — Leading platform design, setting sovereignty standards
- **CALD communities** (Culturally and Linguistically Diverse) — African Australian, Pasifika, Southeast Asian, Middle Eastern young people and families
- **Remote non-Indigenous communities** — Regional and remote towns with limited service access
- **Disability communities** — Young people with cognitive, physical, mental health disabilities
- **LGBTQIA+ communities** — Gender diverse and sexuality diverse young people in youth justice
- **Mainstream services** — Government, NGO, and community practitioners serving all of the above

**Why start with 6 Indigenous communities in Year 1?**
- Highest statistical need (17x overrepresentation)
- Strongest existing relationships (Bourke, Moree, Alice Springs)
- Data sovereignty frameworks most developed
- Other communities join Years 2-3 as capacity scales

## Community Ownership Pathway

### Phase 1: Foundation (Years 1-2)
- A Curious Tractor owns and operates platform
- First Nations Advisory Council governs protocols
- Communities engage as partners

### Phase 2: Transition (Year 3)
- Community Governance Board operational
- Legal structure designed (ACCO incorporation)
- Communities train to manage platform
- Revenue models proven

### Phase 3: Ownership Transfer (Years 4-5)
- Platform legally transfers to community-governed ACCO
- A Curious Tractor becomes service provider (not owner)
- Communities control all decisions
- Revenue flows to Community Investment Fund

### Phase 4: Self-Determination (Year 6+)
- Communities own and govern platform permanently
- Revenue sustains operations (60-70% self-funded)
- Can say "no" to inappropriate funding
- True economic self-determination achieved

### Youth Justice AND Early Childhood Prevention:
- **Intergenerational trauma prevention:** Supporting youth today prevents trauma for tomorrow's 0-5 year olds
- **Longitudinal evidence:** Track which 0-5 programs prevent later justice involvement

Youth justice communities need this infrastructure **regardless** of 0-5 linkages. The project stands on its own merit—the early childhood connection is a **strategic bonus**, not the foundation.

- Exists **forever**, owned by communities

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|---------------------|
| **Community Consultation Fatigue** | Pay properly, early wins, decision-making power to communities |
| **Technology Adoption** | Mobile-first, offline capability, in-person support |
| **Funding Sustainability** | Diversified revenue from Year 1, conservative projections, capacity building |
| **Cultural Appropriation** | First Nations Advisory Council controls all protocols, communities own content |
| **Cybersecurity & Privacy** | ISO-aligned hosting, MFA, annual penetration tests, 24-hour breach response |
| **Safeguarding & Duty of Care** | Trauma-informed practice, clinical supervision, clear escalation protocols |

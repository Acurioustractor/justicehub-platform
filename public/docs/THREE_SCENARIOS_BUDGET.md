## Community Payment Model (All Scenarios)

All scenarios use a **simple, transparent "pay-per-engagement" model** with three components:

> **üí° Example:** A community in the BASE scenario receives approximately **$120K per year** through the combination of partnership fee ($80K) + knowledge holder sessions ($16-20K) + content bounties ($20-40K). This values Indigenous knowledge at consultant rates.

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ Annual Partnership Fee

**One payment per community per year covering base engagement:**

- Local community coordinator retainer (paid monthly)
- Monthly check-ins and relationship management
- Basic equipment and technology support
- Cultural protocol costs
- Administrative coordination

| Scenario | Partnership Fee per Community | What Community Gets |
|----------|------------------------------|---------------------|
| **LEAN** | $60K/year | Part-time coordinator (1-2 days/week), basic support |
| **BASE** | $80K/year | Regular coordinator (2 days/week), solid support |
| **UPPER** | $100K/year | Strong coordinator (3 days/week), comprehensive support |

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ Knowledge Holder Session Fees

**Separate per-session payments directly to Elders, cultural practitioners, and program experts:**

| Scenario | Rate per Session | Logic | Typical Sessions/Year |
|----------|------------------|-------|----------------------|
| **LEAN** | $750 | $150/hour √ó 5 hours | 16 sessions |
| **BASE** | $1,000 | $200/hour √ó 5 hours | 16-20 sessions |
| **UPPER** | $1,200 | $240/hour √ó 5 hours | 20-24 sessions |

> **Why separate from partnership fee?** Makes compensation transparent. Individual knowledge holders see exactly what they're paid for their expertise.

### 3Ô∏è‚É£ Content Creation Bounties

**Pay-per-piece for specific deliverables:**

| Content Type | LEAN | BASE | UPPER | What's Included |
|--------------|------|------|-------|-----------------|
| **Video story** (community-produced) | $2,000 | $2,500 | $3,000 | Filming, editing, cultural review, permissions |
| **Written story** | $500 | $600 | $750 | Interviews, writing, editing, cultural protocols |
| **Program documentation** | $800 | $1,000 | $1,200 | Full program write-up with implementation guide |
| **Podcast interview** | $400 | $500 | $600 | Recording, editing, transcript |

> **Why bounties?** Communities control how much they engage. More content = more payment. Aligns incentives and ensures value-for-money.

> **üí° See Example:** Visit the [Stories page](/stories) to see published stories that communities would be paid to create. Each story includes video/audio, transcript, and cultural context‚Äîrepresenting the quality we're funding.

## Frequently Asked Questions

> **üí° Quick Reference:** Jump to the questions most relevant to your concerns. Each answer includes context and rationale.

### About Community Payments

**Q: Is $80K per year enough to make it "worth their time" for communities?**

A: In BASE, communities receive approximately $120K per year when you include partnership fee ($80K) + knowledge holder sessions ($16-20K) + content bounties ($20-40K). This is substantial compensation that values Indigenous knowledge at consultant rates.

**Q: How do knowledge holders actually receive payment?**

A: Payments go directly to individuals or are managed through community-controlled organisations. Each community decides their own payment distribution model. The partnership fee goes to the community organisation; session fees and content bounties can go directly to individuals.

**Q: What if a community wants to engage more/less than budgeted?**

A: The pay-per-engagement model is flexible. Communities can do more sessions or create more content and receive additional payment. Conversely, if life circumstances require reduced engagement, they're not penalized - they simply do fewer paid sessions.

### About Indigenous Trainees

**Q: Why only 2 trainees in Year 1 for BASE?**

A: Starting with 2 allows proper mentorship and curriculum development. Quality over quantity. We scale to 4 in Year 2 once the training program is proven and running smoothly.

**Q: Is $3,000 per month livable?**

A: In BASE, trainees receive $36K annually ($3K/month) which is above minimum wage and allows focused learning without financial stress. This increases in Year 2-3 before transitioning to paid employment at $45K part-time.

**Q: What happens if trainees don't complete the program?**

A: The program is designed for success with strong mentorship, cultural support, and clear progression. If someone needs to leave, we recruit replacement trainees. The budget includes some buffer for this contingency.

### About Platform & Technology

**Q: Can you really build a quality platform for $500K?**

A: Yes. We're building on existing foundation ($183K already invested). $500K for 12 months gets you solid Next.js platform, proper UX, security compliance, and testing. We're not building Facebook - we're building a focused program directory and story library with Indigenous data sovereignty.

**Q: What if platform costs more than budgeted?**

A: Each scenario has operational contingency (5-7%). Platform work is phased - if costs increase, we reduce scope in Year 1 and continue in Year 2. The budget is realistic based on actual development costs.

**Q: Why does UPPER cost more for platform?**

A: UPPER includes advanced features (AI-powered matching, custom dashboards), PWA for offline capability, more extensive security testing, and professional UX research with all communities. BASE gets you quality; UPPER gets you excellence.

### About A Curious Tractor Fee

**Q: Why does ACT get 8% fee?**

A: This covers ACT's organisational costs to deliver a complex multi-community project: senior project management, financial compliance, governance oversight, risk management, HR support, and organisational infrastructure. 8% is standard for nonprofit complex project management.

**Q: Is this fee negotiable?**

A: The 8% is calculated based on ACT's actual costs to sustainably deliver this work. Going lower risks ACT's organisational sustainability and delivery quality. The fee is transparent and consistent across all scenarios.

**Q: What happens to ACT fee when project transitions to community ownership?**

A: By Year 3-4, ACT transitions from owner/operator to fee-for-service provider. The community-owned organisation may continue contracting ACT for specific services, or may hire their own staff, or may use other providers. ACT doesn't lock in dependency.

### About Sustainability

**Q: Why does revenue take so long to grow?**

A: Social enterprises need 2-3 years to prove value before organisations pay for memberships or training. Government training contracts take 18-24 months from first conversation to signed agreement. We're being realistic, not over-promising quick sustainability.

**Q: What if revenue grows faster than projected?**

A: Great! Faster revenue means less philanthropic dependency. Excess revenue goes to the Community Investment Fund to support community-led projects. Revenue projections are conservative - we may exceed them.

**Q: Is 50-70% self-sustaining realistic for a social impact organisation?**

A: Yes, but it takes 7-10 years. Many successful social enterprises operate at 50-70% earned income with some philanthropic funding for innovation and community programs. 100% self-sustaining often means compromising community-first mission for commercial viability.

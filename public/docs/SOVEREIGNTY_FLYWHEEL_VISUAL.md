## Visual Concept: "The Journey to Community Sovereignty"

**Format:** Circular flywheel with integrated 3-year journey map and systems connections

**Design Philosophy:**
- Combines **flywheel** (self-reinforcing cycle) + **journey map** (3-year progression) + **systems map** (interconnections)
- Uses **circular Indigenous thinking** (not linear Western model)
- Shows **momentum building** toward community ownership
- **Culturally appropriate** - water metaphor (life-giving, flowing, connecting)

## Detailed Design Specifications

### Overall Shape: **Concentric Circles (Like a Ripple in Water)**

**Visual Metaphor:** Water rippling outward from a stone dropped in a pond
- Center stone = Community Sovereignty (the catalyst)
- Inner ripples = The Flywheel (continuous movement)
- Outer ripples = Impact spreading outward
- Water itself = Indigenous knowledge flowing

### Color Palette

**Center (Community Sovereignty):**
- Deep blue-green (life-giving water)
- Gold accents (value, richness)

**Flywheel Ring:**
- Flowing gradient from ochre â†’ terracotta â†’ deep red (earth tones, Indigenous palette)
- Each step a slightly different shade showing progression
- Arrows showing circular flow (not rigid boxes)

**Journey Timeline Ring:**
- Year 1: Lighter tones (beginning, sunrise)
- Year 2: Mid tones (growth, midday)
- Year 3: Deeper tones (maturity, sunset leading to new dawn)

**Budget Paths:**
- LEAN: Thin line (conservative path)
- BASE: Medium bold line (recommended, visible)
- UPPER: Thick line (comprehensive)
- All converge on center (all lead to sovereignty)

### Typography

**Center:** Large, bold - "COMMUNITY SOVEREIGNTY"
**Flywheel Steps:** Clear, numbered, easy to follow
**Journey Timeline:** Medium weight, chronological
**Annotations:** Smaller, supportive detail

### Icons/Symbols

**Communities:** Circle of people (connected)
**Indigenous Developers:** Growing tree (2 saplings â†’ 4 trees showing growth)
**Platform:** Digital layers (building blocks)
**Knowledge:** Water droplets/waves (flowing, life-giving)
**Revenue:** Circular arrows (cyclical, sustainable)
**Impact:** Ripples spreading outward

## Why This Visual Works

### 1. **It's Circular** (Indigenous Thinking)
- Not linear Western logic model
- Shows interconnection, cyclical nature
- Represents sustainability (what goes around, comes around)

### 2. **It Shows the Flywheel** (Momentum)
- Self-reinforcing system (not dependent on grants forever)
- Each step strengthens the next
- Builds toward inevitability of sovereignty

### 3. **It Shows the Journey** (3-Year Progression)
- Clear timeline with milestones
- Transformation visible (trainees â†’ developers)
- Costs reduce over time (Tech Lead eliminated)

### 4. **It Shows Choices** (Budget Scenarios)
- Three paths, all valid, all lead to same goal
- Difference is scale and speed, not direction
- Empowers funder to choose

### 5. **It Connects Everything**
- The Why (top) â†’ The Goal (center) â†’ The Impact (bottom)
- The How (flywheel) â†’ The Journey (timeline)
- What's Built (left) â† â†’ What's Funded (right)

### 6. **It's One Page**
- Can be printed, projected, shared
- Works as PDF, poster, slide
- Tells complete story at a glance

### 7. **It's Beautiful**
- Circular is aesthetically pleasing
- Water metaphor is life-affirming
- Colors are Indigenous-appropriate
- Professional yet culturally grounded

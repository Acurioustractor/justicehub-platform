A shipping container is about to tour Australia.

I want to be honest with you.

CONTAINED is a shipping container, three rooms, and a growing body of evidence that says everything about youth justice in this country needs to change. The vision is simple. Take this thing around Australia and make it impossible to look away.

Decision makers, young people, philanthropists, and everyone influencing this system walks through it. Then they take what they felt back into their rooms, their board rooms, and their reports.

We are building it as we go because the people who need to see it cannot wait for perfect.

One shipping container. Three rooms. Thirty minutes. Touring Australia.

The first room is designed by young people who have experienced the system. They decide what you see. They decide what you feel.

The second room shows you what we saw when we visited Diagrama Foundation in Spain. 13.6% reoffending. 1:1 staffing. Family contact every week. Young people who look like children. Not inmates. Children.

The third room belongs to the local community. At every stop, a local organisation running community-based work that needs eyes, ears, and funds. The people already doing it. Underfunded, unreported, and invisible to the people making policy decisions.
